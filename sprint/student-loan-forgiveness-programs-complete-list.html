Every Student Loan Forgiveness Program Available in 2026

Student loan forgiveness is real, but the landscape is confusing. There are federal programs, state programs, employer programs, and career-specific programs, each with different requirements. This is the most comprehensive list available, updated for 2026.

Federal Forgiveness Programs

Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)

The biggest program. After 120 qualifying payments (10 years) while working full-time for a qualifying employer (government, nonprofit, tribal organizations), your remaining balance is forgiven tax-free. The approval rate has improved dramatically to over 56% in 2026 thanks to program reforms.

Qualifying employers include federal, state, and local government agencies, 501(c)(3) nonprofits, military service, public schools and universities, and public hospitals.

Income-Driven Repayment (IDR) Forgiveness

After 20-25 years of income-driven payments, remaining balances are forgiven. The SAVE plan (newest IDR option) offers the lowest payments at 5-10% of discretionary income with forgiveness after 20 years for undergraduate loans and 25 years for graduate loans.

Teacher Loan Forgiveness

Teachers in low-income schools for 5 consecutive years can receive up to $17,500 in forgiveness for STEM and special education teachers, or $5,000 for other qualifying teachers. This can be combined with PSLF for additional forgiveness after 10 years.

Total and Permanent Disability (TPD) Discharge

Borrowers who are totally and permanently disabled can have their entire federal student loan balance discharged. Requires documentation from a physician, the VA, or the SSA.

Career-Specific Programs

  • Nurses: NURSE Corps Loan Repayment Program — up to 85% forgiveness for working in critical shortage facilities for 3 years
  • Doctors: National Health Service Corps — up to $50,000 for 2 years in underserved areas, renewable
  • Lawyers: Department of Justice Attorney Student Loan Repayment Program — up to $6,000/year for 3 years
  • Military: Each branch offers student loan repayment programs up to $65,000
  • Social Workers: NHSC and state-specific programs for licensed clinical social workers in underserved areas
  • Veterinarians: USDA Veterinary Medicine Loan Repayment Program — up to $25,000/year for 3 years

State-Specific Programs

Nearly every state offers some form of student loan assistance, often tied to working in high-need areas or specific professions. Examples include:

  • New York: Get on Your Feet Loan Forgiveness — up to 24 months of federal loan payments
  • California: State Loan Repayment Program — up to $50,000 for healthcare workers in underserved areas
  • Texas: Multiple programs for teachers, nurses, and attorneys practicing in underserved areas
  • Maine: Opportunity Maine Tax Credit — student loan payments become tax credits

Frequently Asked Questions

What student loan forgiveness programs are available in 2026?

Major programs include Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF), Income-Driven Repayment forgiveness, Teacher Loan Forgiveness, and career-specific programs for nurses, doctors, lawyers, military, and social workers.

How does Public Service Loan Forgiveness work?

Make 120 qualifying payments (10 years) while working full-time for a government or nonprofit employer. The remaining balance is forgiven tax-free. The 2026 approval rate exceeds 56%.

Can teachers get student loan forgiveness?

Yes. Teacher Loan Forgiveness offers up to $17,500 for STEM and special education teachers after 5 years in low-income schools. Teachers can also pursue PSLF for additional forgiveness.

Is student loan forgiveness taxable?

PSLF forgiveness is tax-free. IDR forgiveness was temporarily tax-free through 2025; check current rules for 2026. State tax treatment varies.
