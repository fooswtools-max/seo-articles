Bangkok vs Bali: Which Is Cheaper for Travelers?

The Bottom Line: Bangkok Is Slightly Cheaper

Both Bangkok and Bali are incredible budget destinations, but Bangkok edges ahead on overall value. Bangkok's street food is cheaper, public transport costs almost nothing, and mid-range hotels offer better value. Bali wins on villa-style accommodation and spa treatments. Here is the full breakdown.

Where Bangkok Wins

Food ($8-15/day vs $10-20)

Bangkok's street food is legendary and incredibly cheap. A plate of pad thai costs $1 to $2. A full meal at a local restaurant runs $2 to $4. Even mid-range restaurants rarely exceed $10 per person. Bali's food is good but slightly more expensive, especially in tourist areas like Seminyak where prices match Western levels.

Transport ($2-5/day vs $5-15)

Bangkok's BTS Skytrain and MRT subway cost under $1 per ride. Grab taxis are $2 to $5 for most trips. In Bali, there is no public transit — you need to rent a scooter ($4-5/day) or use Grab/taxi for everything. Scooter rental is the cheapest option but requires driving experience on chaotic roads.

Shopping

Bangkok has Chatuchak Weekend Market (15,000+ stalls), MBK Center, and Pratunam — all significantly cheaper than Bali's shopping options. For clothes, electronics, and souvenirs, Bangkok is unbeatable.

Where Bali Wins

Accommodation (Villas)

In Bali, you can rent a private villa with a pool for $30 to $60 per night — something impossible in Bangkok at that price. For couples wanting a romantic, luxurious stay on a budget, Bali delivers extraordinary value. Areas like Ubud and Amed offer stunning villas at half the price of Seminyak.

Wellness and Spas

A one-hour Balinese massage costs $8 to $15 in Bali. In Bangkok, Thai massage is $6 to $12 — cheaper, but Bali's spa culture offers more variety. Yoga retreats, meditation centers, and wellness resorts are Bali specialties.

Nature and Beaches

Bali has beaches, rice terraces, volcanoes, and waterfalls. Bangkok is a city — for beaches, you need to travel to Koh Samet (2 hours) or the islands (flights). If nature is your priority, Bali wins easily.

Best Time to Visit Each

  • Bangkok cheapest: May to October (rainy season, but rains are brief afternoon showers)
  • Bali cheapest: November to March (wet season, but mornings are usually clear)
  • Avoid for both: December/January (peak prices) and July/August (European summer rush)

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Bangkok or Bali cheaper?

Bangkok is slightly cheaper overall ($40-70/day vs $45-80/day). Bangkok wins on food and transport; Bali wins on villa accommodation.

How much does a week in Bangkok cost?

$280 to $500 mid-range. Budget travelers can manage on $25-35 per day.

How much does a week in Bali cost?

$315 to $560 mid-range. Budget travelers can get by on $30-40 per day outside tourist hotspots.

Which is better for first-time travelers?

Bangkok — easier navigation with BTS/MRT, wider food options, better infrastructure. Bali is better for beaches and a slower pace.

When is the cheapest time to visit?

Bangkok: May-October. Bali: November-March. Avoid Dec/Jan and Jul/Aug for both.
