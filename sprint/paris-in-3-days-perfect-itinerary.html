Paris in 3 Days — The Perfect 2026 Itinerary (With Prices)

3 Days in Paris: A Practical Plan

Three days is enough to experience the best of Paris if you plan well. This itinerary balances the must-see landmarks with neighborhood exploration, local food, and strategic timing to avoid the worst crowds. Every price listed is current for 2026.

Day 1: Icons and the Seine

Morning — Eiffel Tower (8:30 AM): Book the first entry slot online (€29.40 summit by lift, €11.30 stairs to 2nd floor). Morning light is best for photos and crowds are manageable at opening. Budget 2 hours.

Late Morning — Walk to Trocadero: Cross the Pont d'Iena for the classic Eiffel Tower photo from Trocadero plaza. Free.

Lunch — Rue Cler Market Street: A charming pedestrian market street in the 7th arrondissement. Grab a croque monsieur and fresh fruit. Budget €10-15.

Afternoon — Musee d'Orsay (1:30 PM): The world's finest Impressionist collection. Less crowded than the Louvre and more manageable in size. Tickets €16. Budget 2-3 hours.

Evening — Seine River Walk: Walk along the Seine from Orsay to Notre-Dame (under restoration, impressive from outside). Stop at Shakespeare and Company bookshop. End with dinner in the Latin Quarter. Budget €20-30 for dinner.

Day 2: The Louvre and Montmartre

Morning — The Louvre (9 AM): Book online (€22) and enter through the Passage Richelieu entrance (shorter line than the pyramid). Focus on the Big Three (Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, Winged Victory) then explore one wing in depth. Budget 3-4 hours.

Lunch — Le Marais: Walk east to this trendy neighborhood. Falafel on Rue des Rosiers (€7-8) or a bistro lunch menu (€15-18). Browse vintage shops and galleries.

Afternoon — Montmartre (3 PM): Metro to Abbesses station. Walk up through the charming streets to Sacre-Coeur basilica (free entry). Watch street artists in Place du Tertre. The view from the basilica steps is one of the best free views in Paris.

Evening — Montmartre dinner: Avoid tourist traps on the main square. Walk two blocks in any direction for authentic bistros. Try duck confit or steak frites. Budget €25-35.

Day 3: Versailles or Neighborhood Paris

Option A — Versailles Day Trip: RER C train (€7.60 round trip) to the Palace of Versailles (€21). Go at opening (9 AM) and head straight to the gardens before doubling back to the palace. The Hall of Mirrors before 10:30 AM is magical. Budget the full day.

Option B — Neighborhood Exploration:

Morning at Sainte-Chapelle (€11.50 — the most stunning stained glass in Paris) and Ile de la Cite. Afternoon in Saint-Germain-des-Pres for bookshops, cafes, and the Luxembourg Gardens (free). Evening food tour of the 11th arrondissement (self-guided, budget €30-40).

Essential Paris Tips

  • Buy a Navigo Easy card for metro rides (€2.15 each) — cheaper than individual tickets
  • Most museums are free on the first Sunday of the month (expect crowds)
  • Tipping is not expected in restaurants — service is included (service compris)
  • Learn three French phrases: Bonjour, Merci, and Parlez-vous anglais? Parisians appreciate the effort.
  • Book all major attractions online at least 3 days in advance to avoid sold-out situations

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does 3 days in Paris cost?

Budget approximately €225-320 for attractions, food, and transportation over 3 days, excluding accommodation. Mid-range hotels add €150-250 per night.

What should I see in Paris in 3 days?

Day 1: Eiffel Tower, Musee d'Orsay, Seine walk. Day 2: The Louvre, Le Marais, Montmartre and Sacre-Coeur. Day 3: Versailles day trip or Sainte-Chapelle and neighborhood exploration.

Is the Paris Museum Pass worth it for 3 days?

The 2-day pass (€52) covers the Louvre, Orsay, Versailles, and Sainte-Chapelle. If you visit 3+ museums, it saves money and lets you skip ticket lines.

What is the best way to get around Paris?

The Metro is fast, cheap, and covers all major attractions. Buy a Navigo Easy card and load single rides at €2.15 each. Walking between nearby sites is often faster and more scenic.
