How Much Does It Cost to File in Small Claims Court?

Filing fees vary widely by state — from as low as $15 in some states to over $200 in others. The fee usually depends on how much money you are claiming. Most states use a tiered fee structure where higher claims cost more to file.

Understanding these costs upfront helps you decide whether small claims court is worthwhile for your situation. The good news: if you win, the judge will usually order the other party to pay your filing fees back.

How to Reduce Your Filing Costs

  • Apply for a fee waiver if you qualify based on income. Courts cannot deny you access to justice because of inability to pay.
  • Choose the right court location. Some counties within the same state charge different amounts.
  • File online where available. Some states offer reduced fees for electronic filing versus paper filing.
  • Consider mediation first. Many courts offer free mediation services that can resolve your dispute without the filing fee.

Additional Costs to Plan For

Beyond the filing fee, budget for these potential costs:

  • Service of process: $20-75 to have the defendant officially served with court papers
  • Copies and documentation: $5-20 for copies of evidence and forms
  • Time off work: Most hearings are during business hours and last 30-60 minutes
  • Travel costs: You may need to file in the defendant's county

Is It Worth Filing?

As a general rule, filing in small claims court is worth it when the amount owed is significantly more than the filing fee and service costs combined. If someone owes you $500 and the total filing costs are $80, you stand to recover $580 (your claim plus fees) from the judgment.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average small claims court filing fee?

The average filing fee across all states ranges from $30 to $75 for claims under $5,000. Some states charge more for higher claim amounts, with fees going up to $200 for claims near the state maximum.

Can I get my filing fee waived?

Yes. Most courts offer fee waivers for low-income individuals. You will need to fill out a fee waiver form and provide proof of income or government assistance. Approval is typically granted if your income is below 125-150% of the federal poverty level.

Do I get my filing fee back if I win?

In most states, yes. The judge can order the losing party to reimburse your filing fees as part of the judgment. Make sure to include the filing fee in your total claim amount.

Are there additional costs beyond the filing fee?

You may need to pay for serving the defendant, which costs $20-75 depending on the method. Some courts also charge for requesting a continuance or filing a counterclaim.

Can I file in any state?

You must file in the state and county where the defendant lives, where the incident occurred, or where a contract was signed. You cannot choose just any state for convenience.
