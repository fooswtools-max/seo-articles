Is AliExpress Safe? A Buyer's Complete Guide (2026)

Is AliExpress safe to buy from? Complete 2026 guide covering payment security, buyer protection, how to spot scams, and tips for safe shopping.

Short Answer: Yes, AliExpress Is Safe

AliExpress is a legitimate, safe marketplace owned by Alibaba Group — a Fortune Global 500 company. Over 150 million buyers use it worldwide. Your payment information is protected, and a robust buyer protection system covers your purchases.

That said, like any marketplace, there are some sellers who try to scam buyers. This guide teaches you exactly how to shop safely and avoid problems.

How AliExpress Protects You

Secure Payments

AliExpress holds your payment in escrow until you confirm receipt of your order. The seller does not receive your money until you are satisfied. This means you always have leverage if something goes wrong.

Buyer Protection Program

Every purchase includes buyer protection with these guarantees:

  • Full refund if your order does not arrive within the protection period
  • Full or partial refund if the item is significantly different from the description
  • Free returns on millions of items marked with the free return badge
  • Dispute resolution mediated by AliExpress if you and the seller cannot agree

Payment Security

AliExpress accepts Visa, Mastercard, PayPal, Apple Pay, and Google Pay. Your card details are encrypted and never shared with sellers. The checkout process uses the same security standards as major Western retailers.

How to Spot and Avoid Scams

  • Too good to be true prices: A $10 iPhone is obviously fake. Use common sense on pricing.
  • Brand-name products at huge discounts: These are likely counterfeits. Buy unbranded alternatives instead.
  • New sellers with no reviews: Stick to established sellers with extensive positive feedback.
  • Requests to pay outside AliExpress: Never pay via wire transfer, Western Union, or direct bank transfer. Always use the AliExpress checkout.
  • Suspiciously few product photos: Legitimate sellers show multiple angles and real product photos.

Safety Tip: Use a virtual credit card number for online shopping. Many banks offer this feature for free, and it adds an extra layer of protection by creating a temporary card number for each purchase.

Tips for Safe AliExpress Shopping

  1. Always read reviews with photos. These show the actual product quality, not the seller's professional photos.
  2. Choose sellers with 95%+ positive feedback and at least 1,000 transactions.
  3. Sort by "Orders" to find the most popular (and proven) listings.
  4. Check the buyer protection period and do not confirm receipt until you have inspected the item.
  5. Save screenshots of the product listing in case you need to open a dispute.
  6. Start with small orders to test a new seller before buying expensive items.
  7. Use the AliExpress app — it often has better prices and exclusive deals.

What to Do If Something Goes Wrong

If your order has a problem, open a dispute through the AliExpress app or website. Upload photos or videos showing the issue. AliExpress typically resolves disputes within 7-15 days. Most buyers report successful resolutions, especially when they provide clear photo evidence.

The Verdict

AliExpress is as safe as eBay or Amazon Marketplace when you follow basic precautions. The buyer protection system, secure payments, and escrow process make it a reliable platform. Just stick to well-reviewed sellers, read buyer photos, and use common sense on pricing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is AliExpress a legitimate website?

Yes. AliExpress is owned by Alibaba Group, one of the world's largest companies publicly traded on the NYSE. It has over 150 million active buyers worldwide and has been operating since 2010.

Will AliExpress steal my credit card information?

No. AliExpress uses secure payment processing and never shares your card details with sellers. You can also pay via PayPal or virtual card numbers for extra security. The payment system is comparable to Amazon or eBay.

What if I receive a defective or wrong item?

AliExpress Buyer Protection covers you. Open a dispute within the protection period, upload photos of the issue, and AliExpress will mediate. You can get a full refund or partial refund without returning the item in many cases.

Are AliExpress products counterfeit?

Some sellers do sell counterfeit goods, which violates AliExpress policy. Avoid listings that use brand names at suspiciously low prices. Stick to unbranded items or verified brand stores for the safest experience.

How do I know if an AliExpress seller is trustworthy?

Check the seller's feedback score, years in business, and number of transactions. Look for sellers with 95% or higher positive feedback and over 1,000 transactions. Read recent buyer reviews with photos.
