How to File Small Claims Court Online in 2026 — Step by Step Guide

Filing Small Claims Court Online Has Never Been Easier

Small claims court is designed for ordinary people to resolve disputes without hiring a lawyer. In 2026, many states allow partial or full online filing, and AI-powered tools can generate your court documents in minutes. Here is exactly how the process works.

Step 1: Check Your State's Small Claims Limit

Every state has a maximum dollar amount for small claims. In California, it is $12,500. In New York, $10,000. In Texas, $20,000. Most states fall between $5,000 and $15,000. If your claim exceeds the limit, you can either sue for the maximum (waiving the excess) or file in a higher court.

The most common small claims cases are security deposit disputes (the number one case type), unpaid debts, property damage, consumer complaints, and breach of contract.

Step 2: Gather Your Evidence

Strong evidence wins small claims cases. Collect everything before filing:

  • Contracts or agreements (even text messages count)
  • Receipts and invoices showing amounts paid or owed
  • Photos and videos documenting damage or conditions
  • Written communication — emails, texts, letters showing the dispute
  • Witness contact information for anyone who observed the situation
  • Timeline of events showing what happened and when

Step 3: File Your Claim

Many states now offer online filing through their court websites. You will need the defendant's full legal name, address, and the amount you are claiming. Filing fees range from $30 to $75 in most states, and you can usually recover this fee if you win.

If your state does not offer online filing, you can file in person at your local courthouse. Bring two copies of your completed claim form.

Step 4: Serve the Defendant

The defendant must be formally notified of the lawsuit. Options include certified mail, a process server ($30-75), or the sheriff's office ($25-50). You cannot serve the papers yourself. Service must be completed a specific number of days before the court date (usually 15-30 days).

Step 5: Prepare and Present Your Case

Small claims hearings are informal. No lawyer is needed. The judge will ask you to explain what happened, show your evidence, and state what you want. Keep it factual, organized, and brief. Bring originals of all documents plus copies for the judge and the defendant.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I file small claims court online?

Many states now offer online filing through their court websites. Check your local court's website for e-filing options. Even where online filing is not available, AI tools can generate your documents for in-person filing.

How much does it cost to file small claims court?

Filing fees range from $30 to $75 in most states. You can typically recover this fee from the defendant if you win your case.

Do I need a lawyer for small claims court?

No. Small claims court is designed for self-representation. In some states, lawyers are not even permitted. You present your own case directly to the judge.

What is the small claims court limit in my state?

Limits vary by state: California $12,500, New York $10,000, Texas $20,000, Florida $8,000. Most states fall between $5,000 and $15,000. Check your specific state's limit before filing.

How long does a small claims case take?

From filing to hearing is typically 30 to 60 days. The hearing itself usually lasts 15 to 30 minutes. Judgments are often issued the same day or within a few weeks.
