Best Free Things to Do in Amsterdam (2026) — 15 Activities That Cost Nothing

Amsterdam Without Spending a Euro

Amsterdam has a reputation as an expensive city, but some of its best experiences cost absolutely nothing. Skip the €22 museum tickets for a day and explore these genuinely free activities that locals love.

1-5: Outdoor Experiences

1. Vondelpark — Amsterdam's 47-hectare Central Park equivalent. Free open-air concerts in summer, beautiful rose garden, and perfect for people-watching. Pack a picnic from Albert Heijn.

2. Free Ferry to Amsterdam Noord — Behind Central Station, free ferries cross the IJ river every 5-10 minutes. NDSM Wharf on the north side has street art, food trucks, and a creative warehouse district.

3. Begijnhof — A hidden medieval courtyard in the city center. Step through an unmarked door off Spui square into a serene 14th-century enclave with the oldest wooden house in Amsterdam (circa 1420).

4. Albert Cuyp Market — Amsterdam's largest street market (300+ stalls) is free to browse. Sample Dutch stroopwafels, Indonesian snacks, and cheese. Open Monday-Saturday.

5. Jordaan Neighborhood Walk — The most photogenic neighborhood in Amsterdam. Narrow canals, independent boutiques, tiny bridges, and hidden hofjes (courtyard gardens). Free to explore, impossible not to photograph.

6-10: Culture and History

6. Civic Guard Gallery at Amsterdam Museum — A free glass-enclosed gallery connecting two streets, displaying enormous 17th-century group portraits of Amsterdam's militia companies.

7. Openbare Bibliotheek (Public Library) — The OBA central library near Central Station has a stunning rooftop terrace with panoramic city views. Completely free.

8. Street Art in NDSM Wharf — Take the free ferry north and explore Europe's largest collection of urban street art. Massive murals, graffiti, and installations cover old shipyard warehouses.

9. Bloemenmarkt (Flower Market) — The world's only floating flower market on the Singel canal. Free to browse, and the tulip bulb displays are Instagram gold.

10. EYE Film Museum Basement — The exhibition spaces upstairs cost money, but the free basement area has interactive film installations and panoramic cafe views across the IJ river.

11-15: Hidden Gems

11. Noordermarkt Saturday — Organic farmers market on Saturday mornings. Free live music, artisan food sampling, and the best apple pie in Amsterdam from nearby Winkel 43.

12. Skinny Bridge at Night — The Magere Brug (Skinny Bridge) over the Amstel River illuminated at night is one of Amsterdam's most romantic free views.

13. Westerpark — A post-industrial park with a converted gasworks building (Westergasfabriek), monthly Sunday markets, and free festivals throughout summer.

14. Dam Square and Royal Palace Exterior — The heart of Amsterdam. Street performers, the National Monument, and the Royal Palace facade are all free to enjoy.

15. Canal Ring Walk at Golden Hour — Walk along Herengracht, Keizersgracht, and Prinsengracht during sunset. The light reflecting off canal water and gabled houses is Amsterdam at its finest.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best free things to do in Amsterdam?

Top free activities include Vondelpark, the free ferry to NDSM Wharf, Begijnhof courtyard, Albert Cuyp Market, Jordaan neighborhood walk, and the public library rooftop terrace.

Is the Amsterdam ferry free?

Yes, the ferries behind Central Station to Amsterdam Noord are completely free and run every 5-10 minutes throughout the day.

Are there free museums in Amsterdam?

The Civic Guard Gallery at the Amsterdam Museum entrance is free. The EYE Film Museum basement has free interactive exhibits. Most major museums charge €15-22.

What is the best free market in Amsterdam?

Albert Cuyp Market is the largest with 300+ stalls, open Monday through Saturday. Noordermarkt on Saturday mornings is best for organic food and local atmosphere.
