AliExpress Buyer Protection: Everything You Need to Know

Complete guide to AliExpress Buyer Protection in 2026. Learn how disputes work, refund policies, what's covered, and how to protect yourself as a buyer.

Your Money Is Protected on Every Purchase

AliExpress Buyer Protection is one of the strongest buyer protection programs in e-commerce. It uses an escrow system where your payment is held by AliExpress — not sent to the seller — until you confirm that your order arrived and is satisfactory.

This means you always have leverage, and the seller has strong incentive to keep you happy.

How the Escrow System Works

  1. You place an order and pay AliExpress
  2. AliExpress holds your money (does not send it to the seller)
  3. The seller ships your order
  4. You receive and inspect the item
  5. You click "Confirm Receipt" if everything is good
  6. Only then does AliExpress release payment to the seller

Important: Do not click "Confirm Receipt" until you have thoroughly inspected your item. Once confirmed, your buyer protection ends and getting a refund becomes much harder.

What Is Covered

  • Item not received: Full refund if tracking shows no delivery within the protection period
  • Wrong item sent: Full refund or free replacement
  • Item significantly different from description: Full or partial refund with photo evidence
  • Defective or damaged item: Full or partial refund depending on severity
  • Counterfeit product: Full refund — AliExpress takes counterfeits seriously
  • Missing quantity: Partial refund for missing items in a multi-item order

How to Open a Dispute (Step by Step)

  1. Go to "My Orders" in the AliExpress app or website
  2. Find the problematic order and click "Open Dispute"
  3. Select whether you received the item or not
  4. Choose the specific reason for your dispute
  5. Enter the refund amount you are requesting
  6. Upload clear photos or video showing the problem
  7. Write a brief description of the issue
  8. Submit and wait for the seller to respond (3-5 days)

Tips for Winning Disputes

  • Take photos immediately when you open the package — ideally a video of the unboxing
  • Be specific about what is wrong and reference the original listing
  • Stay polite and factual in all communications
  • Escalate to AliExpress if the seller rejects your dispute unfairly
  • Keep all evidence — screenshots of the listing, chat with seller, photos of the item

Protection Period Timeline

Your buyer protection has a countdown timer visible on your order page. Key things to know:

  • The timer starts when the seller ships the item
  • Standard protection is 60 to 90 days
  • You can request an extension if the item has not arrived and the timer is running low
  • Once protection expires and you have not opened a dispute, payment is released to the seller
  • Set a reminder to check your order before protection expires

Free Returns Program

Millions of AliExpress items now include free returns, marked with a "Free Return" badge. For these items, AliExpress covers the return shipping cost if you are not satisfied. Look for this badge when shopping to minimize risk on purchases you are unsure about.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does AliExpress Buyer Protection cover?

Buyer Protection covers non-delivery of items, items significantly different from the description, defective or damaged products, and counterfeit goods. You can get a full refund, partial refund, or free replacement depending on the situation.

How long does AliExpress Buyer Protection last?

The protection period varies by product and shipping method but typically lasts 60 to 90 days from the order date. You can request an extension if your item has not arrived and the protection is about to expire.

How do I open a dispute on AliExpress?

Go to your order, click Open Dispute, select the reason, describe the problem, upload photo or video evidence, and submit. AliExpress will mediate between you and the seller. Most disputes are resolved within 7-15 days.

Will I always get a refund if I open a dispute?

In most cases, yes — if you have evidence. AliExpress favors buyers when there is clear photographic evidence of problems. For non-delivery claims, you automatically get a refund if tracking shows no delivery within the protection period.

Should I contact the seller before opening a dispute?

Yes. Many sellers will offer a quick resolution like a partial refund or replacement without needing a formal dispute. Message the seller first, give them 3-5 days to respond, and open a dispute if they do not resolve it.
