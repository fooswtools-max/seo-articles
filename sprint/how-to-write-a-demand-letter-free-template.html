What Is a Demand Letter and Why You Need One

A demand letter is the most important step before filing a small claims court case. It is a formal, written request to someone who owes you money, asking them to pay by a specific deadline. In many states, judges actually expect to see proof that you sent one before they will hear your case.

Sending a well-crafted demand letter accomplishes several things: it puts the other party on official notice, creates a paper trail for court, and often resolves the dispute without ever needing to file a lawsuit. Studies show that over 30% of disputes are settled after receiving a proper demand letter.

Step-by-Step: How to Write Your Demand Letter

  1. Include your full name and contact information at the top, along with the date and the recipient's full name and address.
  2. State the facts clearly. Describe what happened, when it happened, and how much money is owed. Be specific with dates, amounts, and any agreement that was made.
  3. Reference any supporting evidence you have — contracts, receipts, text messages, photos, or emails. Mention them but do not attach them to the letter.
  4. State the amount you are demanding in clear terms. Include the original debt plus any reasonable additional costs like interest or damages.
  5. Set a deadline for payment. Give them 14 to 30 days to respond or pay. Be specific with the date.
  6. State your intent to file in small claims court if payment is not received by the deadline. This is not a threat — it is a factual statement of your next step.
  7. Keep the tone professional. Avoid emotional language, insults, or threats. Judges may read this letter, and a calm, factual tone works in your favor.

Free Demand Letter Template

Template Structure:

[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[Date]

[Recipient Name]
[Recipient Address]

RE: Demand for Payment — [Amount Owed]

Dear [Recipient Name],

This letter serves as formal demand for payment of $[AMOUNT] owed to me for [DESCRIBE REASON — services rendered, property damage, unpaid rent, etc.] on [DATE OF INCIDENT/AGREEMENT].

[DESCRIBE THE FACTS: what was agreed, what happened, what evidence you have.]

I am requesting full payment of $[AMOUNT] by [DEADLINE DATE — 14-30 days from letter date]. Payment can be made by [CHECK/MONEY ORDER/BANK TRANSFER] to [YOUR PAYMENT DETAILS].

If I do not receive payment by the stated deadline, I intend to file a claim in small claims court to recover the amount owed, plus court filing fees and any additional damages allowed by law.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
[Your Signature]

How to Send Your Demand Letter

Always send your demand letter via USPS Certified Mail with Return Receipt Requested. This costs about $7-8 and gives you proof that the letter was delivered. Keep a copy of everything — the letter, the certified mail receipt, and the green return receipt card.

You can also send a courtesy copy via email, but the certified mail version is what matters in court. Take a photo of the sealed envelope before mailing for additional documentation.

What to Do After Sending

Mark your calendar for the deadline date. If the recipient pays, get the payment in a verifiable form and send a written confirmation. If they do not respond or refuse to pay, you are ready to file your small claims court case with the demand letter as evidence.

Many courts have online filing systems that let you submit your case from home. Your demand letter, along with proof of delivery, becomes Exhibit A in your case file.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a demand letter?

A demand letter is a formal written notice sent to someone who owes you money or has caused you harm. It outlines what they owe, why they owe it, and sets a deadline for payment before you take legal action in small claims court.

Do I need to send a demand letter before small claims court?

While not always legally required, most judges expect to see that you attempted to resolve the dispute first. A demand letter shows good faith and can strengthen your case significantly.

How long should I give someone to respond to a demand letter?

A standard timeframe is 14 to 30 days. This gives the recipient enough time to review the letter, consult with anyone they need to, and arrange payment or propose a resolution.

Can I send a demand letter by email?

Yes, but sending via certified mail with return receipt is stronger evidence in court. If you send by email, also send a physical copy via certified mail for documentation purposes.

What happens if they ignore my demand letter?

If the recipient ignores your demand letter, you can proceed with filing a small claims court case. The ignored letter actually helps your case by showing the judge you tried to resolve it amicably.
