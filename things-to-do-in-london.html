Best Things to Do in London - 2026 Guide

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

London is a historic world capital blending royal heritage, world-class museums, and vibrant culture. Whether you are planning your first visit or returning for more, this guide covers the 8 best things to do in London, United Kingdom including prices, tips, and what to expect at each attraction.

Top 8 Things to Do in London

1. Tower of London (~$33)

Historic castle housing the Crown Jewels and 900 years of history. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

2. London Eye (~$32)

Giant observation wheel with 360-degree views of the London skyline. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

3. British Museum (Free)

World-class collection including the Rosetta Stone and Parthenon Marbles. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

4. Buckingham Palace (~$30)

Royal residence with State Rooms open in summer (Changing of the Guard is free). Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

5. Westminster Abbey (~$27)

Gothic abbey where monarchs are crowned and notable Britons are buried. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

6. Natural History Museum (Free)

Stunning Victorian building with dinosaur skeletons and precious gems. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

7. Warner Bros. Studio Tour (~$53)

Behind-the-scenes Harry Potter experience with original sets and props. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

8. Thames River Cruise (~$15)

Scenic boat ride past Big Ben, Tower Bridge, and the Globe Theatre. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit London is May-September when weather is pleasant and crowds are manageable.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Look for a London city pass that bundles popular attractions at a discount
  • Book skip-the-line tickets online to save both time and money
  • Visit free attractions and parks to balance your budget
  • Eat at local spots away from the main tourist streets

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the top free things to do in London?

London has many free world-class museums including the British Museum, National Gallery, Tate Modern, Natural History Museum, and Victoria and Albert Museum. Walking along the South Bank, visiting Hyde Park, and watching the Changing of the Guard are also free.

How many days do you need in London?

4-5 days is ideal. This covers major landmarks, museums, a show in the West End, and day trips to Stonehenge or Windsor Castle.

Is London expensive for tourists?

London can be expensive but many top attractions are free. Budget travelers can eat well at markets like Borough Market, use the Oyster card for transport, and enjoy free museums. A mid-range daily budget is $120-250.
