Best Things to Do in Singapore - 2026 Guide

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Singapore is a futuristic city-state blending high-tech architecture, diverse cuisine, and tropical gardens. Whether you are planning your first visit or returning for more, this guide covers the 7 best things to do in Singapore, Singapore including prices, tips, and what to expect at each attraction.

Top 7 Things to Do in Singapore

1. Gardens by the Bay (~$20)

Futuristic park with Supertree Grove (free) and Cloud Forest dome. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

2. Marina Bay Sands SkyPark (~$26)

Iconic hotel rooftop with infinity pool views (observation deck open to public). Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

3. Sentosa Island (Free)

Resort island with Universal Studios, beaches, and cable car rides. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

4. Singapore Zoo (~$35)

Award-winning zoo with open-concept exhibits and Night Safari. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

5. Chinatown (Free)

Historic district with temples, street art, and legendary hawker food. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

6. ArtScience Museum (~$19)

Lotus-shaped museum with rotating exhibitions and permanent digital galleries. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

7. Hawker Center Food Tour (~$10)

Self-guided tour of Michelin-starred hawker stalls for $3-5 per dish. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Singapore is February-April (driest months) when weather is pleasant and crowds are manageable.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Look for a Singapore city pass that bundles popular attractions at a discount
  • Book skip-the-line tickets online to save both time and money
  • Visit free attractions and parks to balance your budget
  • Eat at local spots away from the main tourist streets

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the must-see attractions in Singapore?

Top attractions include Gardens by the Bay (Supertree Grove is free), Marina Bay Sands, Sentosa Island, Chinatown and Little India, the Singapore Botanic Gardens (free, UNESCO site), and a hawker center food tour.

Is Singapore expensive?

Singapore can be moderate. While hotels and taxis are pricey, hawker center meals cost $3-5, many gardens and temples are free, and public transport (MRT) is affordable. Attractions like Gardens by the Bay offer good value.

How many days do you need in Singapore?

3-4 days is enough to cover the main highlights. The city is compact and efficient, with excellent public transport connecting all major attractions.
