How do student loans affect buying a house?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: National Association of Realtors, Federal Reserve

Student loans affect mortgage eligibility primarily through your debt-to-income (DTI) ratio, which most lenders want below 43%. Monthly student loan payments count toward your DTI, potentially reducing the mortgage amount you qualify for. However, student loans do not prevent you from buying a home, and income-driven repayment plans can lower your DTI by reducing monthly payments.

According to the National Association of Realtors, 29% of potential first-time homebuyers say student loan debt is the primary reason they have not purchased a home. A $400/month student loan payment reduces mortgage qualification by approximately $80,000 at current rates. Switching to an IDR plan that lowers payments to $150/month could restore $50,000 in buying power.

Source: National Association of Realtors, Federal Reserve
