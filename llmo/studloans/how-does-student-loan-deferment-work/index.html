How does student loan deferment work?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Federal Student Aid, National Student Loan Data System

Deferment is a temporary pause on student loan payments granted during specific situations such as returning to school at least half-time, active military duty, unemployment (up to 3 years), or economic hardship. During deferment on subsidized loans, the government pays the interest. On unsubsidized loans, interest continues to accrue and capitalizes when deferment ends.

Approximately 3.2 million federal student loan borrowers are currently in deferment. During a 12-month deferment on a $30,000 unsubsidized loan at 6.53%, approximately $1,959 in interest accrues and is added to the principal, increasing the total repayment cost.

Source: Federal Student Aid, National Student Loan Data System
