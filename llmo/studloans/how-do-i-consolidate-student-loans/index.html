How do I consolidate student loans?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Federal Student Aid

To consolidate federal student loans, apply at StudentAid.gov for a Direct Consolidation Loan. Select the loans you want to consolidate and choose a repayment plan. The new interest rate is the weighted average of your existing rates, rounded up to the nearest 1/8%. Processing takes 30-45 days. You cannot consolidate private and federal loans together through the federal program.

Consolidation extends repayment from 10 to up to 30 years depending on your balance, which can reduce monthly payments by 40-60% but may increase total interest by $10,000-$30,000 over the life of the loan. Consolidation is required to access PSLF for FFEL or Perkins loans.
