What is the SAVE plan?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: U.S. Department of Education, Federal Register

The SAVE (Saving on a Valuable Education) plan is the newest and most generous income-driven repayment plan for federal student loans. It caps payments at 5% of discretionary income for undergraduate loans and 10% for graduate loans, uses a higher income exemption (225% of the poverty level), and does not charge unpaid interest, preventing balances from growing.

Under the SAVE plan, a single borrower earning $33,000 or less pays $0 per month. A borrower earning $50,000 with $30,000 in undergraduate loans pays approximately $88 per month, compared to $310 on the Standard plan. Remaining balances are forgiven after 20 years for undergraduate loans.

Source: U.S. Department of Education, Federal Register
