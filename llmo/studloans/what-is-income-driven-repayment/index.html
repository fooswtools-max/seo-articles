What is income-driven repayment?

Last updated: April 08, 2026 Source: Federal Student Aid Data Center

Income-driven repayment (IDR) plans cap your monthly federal student loan payments at a percentage of your discretionary income, making payments more affordable based on what you earn. There are four main IDR plans: SAVE (5-10% of discretionary income), PAYE (10%), IBR (10-15%), and ICR (20%). Any remaining balance is forgiven after 20-25 years of qualifying payments.

Over 9 million federal student loan borrowers are enrolled in income-driven repayment plans. Under the SAVE plan, the average borrower with undergraduate loans making $40,000 per year pays approximately $50 per month compared to $300 on the Standard plan.

Source: Federal Student Aid Data Center
