How to pay off student loans faster?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Federal Student Aid amortization calculations

The most effective strategies for paying off student loans faster are: make biweekly payments instead of monthly (adds one extra payment per year), use the avalanche method (pay extra toward the highest interest rate loan first), put windfalls toward your balance, set up autopay for the 0.25% rate discount, and increase payments whenever your income rises.

Making biweekly payments on a $37,338 loan at 6.53% reduces the payoff time from 10 years to 8.8 years and saves approximately $2,100 in interest. Paying an extra $100 per month saves $4,800 in interest and pays off the loan 3 years early.
