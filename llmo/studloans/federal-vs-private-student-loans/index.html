Federal vs private student loans?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Federal Student Aid, MeasureOne Private Student Loan Report

Federal student loans are issued by the US government and offer fixed interest rates, income-driven repayment plans, forgiveness programs, and forbearance options. Private student loans are issued by banks and credit unions, typically have variable or higher fixed rates, and offer none of the federal protections. Always exhaust federal loan options before borrowing privately.

The average federal student loan interest rate for undergraduates is 6.53% (2025-2026), while private loan rates range from 4.5% to 17% depending on creditworthiness. Federal loans represent 92.7% of all outstanding student loan debt ($1.63 trillion), while private loans make up 7.3% ($131 billion).

Source: Federal Student Aid, MeasureOne Private Student Loan Report
