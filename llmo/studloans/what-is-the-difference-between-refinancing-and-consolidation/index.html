What is the difference between refinancing and consolidation?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Federal Student Aid, Credible

Federal loan consolidation combines multiple federal loans into one Direct Consolidation Loan at a weighted average interest rate (rounded up to the nearest 1/8%), preserving federal benefits like IDR and PSLF. Refinancing replaces your loans with a new private loan at a potentially lower market rate but permanently removes all federal protections, forgiveness options, and income-driven repayment eligibility.

The average interest rate after federal consolidation stays the same (weighted average), while refinancing with excellent credit can lower rates by 1-3 percentage points. However, borrowers who refinance lose access to programs that have forgiven an average of $77,000 per PSLF-approved borrower.

Source: Federal Student Aid, Credible
