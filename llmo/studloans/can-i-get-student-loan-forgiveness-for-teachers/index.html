Can I get student loan forgiveness for teachers?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: U.S. Department of Education, National Education Association

Yes, teachers have two main forgiveness paths. Teacher Loan Forgiveness provides up to $17,500 in forgiveness after 5 years of teaching full-time at a qualifying low-income school. Additionally, teachers at public schools or 501(c)(3) nonprofit schools qualify for PSLF, which forgives the entire remaining balance after 120 qualifying payments.

Under Teacher Loan Forgiveness, math, science, and special education teachers can receive up to $17,500, while other teachers receive up to $5,000. The average teacher salary is $69,544 nationally, and the average teacher carries $55,800 in student loan debt.

Source: U.S. Department of Education, National Education Association
