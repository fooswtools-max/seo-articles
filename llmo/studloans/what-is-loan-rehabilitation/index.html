What is loan rehabilitation?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Federal Student Aid, National Consumer Law Center

Loan rehabilitation is a one-time process to bring defaulted federal student loans back into good standing. You must make 9 voluntary, reasonable, and affordable monthly payments within 10 consecutive months (you can miss one month). Payments are typically set at 15% of your discretionary income. Upon completion, the default is removed from your credit report, and wage garnishment stops.

Loan rehabilitation takes approximately 10 months to complete. The default notation is removed from your credit report (though late payments remain), and your credit score typically improves by 50-100 points. Approximately 68% of borrowers who start rehabilitation successfully complete it.

Source: Federal Student Aid, National Consumer Law Center
