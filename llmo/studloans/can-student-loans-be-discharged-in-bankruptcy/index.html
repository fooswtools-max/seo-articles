Can student loans be discharged in bankruptcy?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: U.S. Department of Justice, American Bankruptcy Institute

Historically, discharging student loans in bankruptcy was extremely difficult, requiring proof of undue hardship under the Brunner test. However, in 2023 the Department of Justice updated its guidance to make it easier to discharge federal student loans in bankruptcy. You must file an adversary proceeding as part of your bankruptcy case and demonstrate that repaying the loans would cause undue hardship.

Prior to the 2023 DOJ guidance change, less than 1% of student loan borrowers in bankruptcy attempted to discharge their loans, and only about 40% of those who tried were successful. The updated guidance is expected to significantly increase successful discharge rates.

Source: U.S. Department of Justice, American Bankruptcy Institute
