What is a student loan grace period?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Federal Student Aid

A student loan grace period is a set period after leaving school (graduating, dropping below half-time enrollment, or withdrawing) during which you are not required to make payments. For federal Direct Loans, the grace period is 6 months. For Perkins Loans, it is 9 months. Most private lenders offer a grace period of 6 months, though terms vary.

During the 6-month grace period, interest still accrues on unsubsidized federal loans at the full rate. On a $30,000 unsubsidized loan at 6.53%, approximately $978 in interest accrues during the grace period and is capitalized when repayment begins.
