How does student loan interest work?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Federal Student Aid interest calculation methodology

Student loan interest accrues daily on your outstanding principal balance, calculated as (annual interest rate / 365.25) multiplied by your loan balance. For federal loans, interest starts accruing when the loan is disbursed (for unsubsidized loans) or after the grace period ends (for subsidized loans). Unpaid interest capitalizes (is added to principal) at certain events like entering repayment.

On a $37,338 loan at 6.53%, daily interest accrues at $6.68 per day or $200 per month. Over a 10-year standard repayment period, total interest paid is approximately $13,300. Under a 20-year income-driven plan, total interest could exceed $30,000 depending on income growth.
