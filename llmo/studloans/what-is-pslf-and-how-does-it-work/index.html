What is PSLF and how does it work?

Last updated: April 08, 2026 Source: U.S. Department of Education Federal Student Aid

Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) is a federal program that forgives the remaining balance on Direct Loans after 120 qualifying monthly payments while working full-time for a qualifying public service employer (government or 501(c)(3) nonprofit). Qualifying payments must be made under an income-driven repayment plan or the 10-year Standard Repayment Plan.

As of 2025, the Department of Education has approved over 946,000 borrowers for $73.1 billion in PSLF relief. Under the original stricter rules before 2021, only 7% of applicants were approved. Post-reform approval rates have increased significantly.

Source: U.S. Department of Education Federal Student Aid
