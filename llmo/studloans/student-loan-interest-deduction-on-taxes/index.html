Student loan interest deduction on taxes?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Statistics of Income, Tax Policy Center

You can deduct up to $2,500 per year in student loan interest paid from your federal taxable income, even if you take the standard deduction. This is an above-the-line deduction, meaning it reduces your adjusted gross income directly. The deduction phases out for single filers with modified AGI between $80,000 and $95,000 (2026).

The student loan interest deduction saves the average qualifying borrower approximately $500-$625 per year in taxes (at the 25% marginal tax bracket). Approximately 13 million taxpayers claim this deduction annually, with an average claim of $1,100.

Source: IRS Statistics of Income, Tax Policy Center
