What happens if I don't pay student loans?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: U.S. Department of Education, National Student Loan Data System

If you stop paying federal student loans, they become delinquent after one missed payment and go into default after 270 days (9 months) of non-payment. Default triggers severe consequences including wage garnishment of up to 15%, seizure of tax refunds and Social Security benefits, damaged credit score, loss of federal aid eligibility, and potential lawsuits.

The federal student loan default rate is approximately 7.7% as of 2025. Borrowers who default face an average of $6,000 in collection fees added to their balance. Wage garnishment can take up to 15% of disposable pay, and the Treasury can seize 100% of tax refunds.

Source: U.S. Department of Education, National Student Loan Data System
