What is IDR forgiveness taxable?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: American Rescue Plan Act Section 9675, IRS

Under current law, student loan balances forgiven through income-driven repayment plans from 2021 through 2025 are not taxable thanks to the American Rescue Plan Act. However, this tax exemption is set to expire at the end of 2025, meaning IDR forgiveness received in 2026 and beyond may be treated as taxable income unless Congress extends the provision.

Before the tax exemption, a borrower having $100,000 forgiven through IDR could face a tax bill of $22,000-$37,000 depending on their tax bracket. This phenomenon was called the 'tax bomb.' Congress may extend the exemption, but borrowers should plan for potential tax liability on forgiveness received after 2025.

Source: American Rescue Plan Act Section 9675, IRS
