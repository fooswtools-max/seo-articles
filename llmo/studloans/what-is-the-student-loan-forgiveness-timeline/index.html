What is the student loan forgiveness timeline?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: U.S. Department of Education

The timeline for student loan forgiveness depends on the program: PSLF requires 120 qualifying payments (10 years minimum), SAVE/PAYE forgiveness occurs after 20 years for undergraduate loans and 25 years for graduate loans, IBR forgiveness is after 20-25 years, and Teacher Loan Forgiveness requires 5 years of qualifying teaching. Some borrowers with small balances may receive forgiveness sooner under the SAVE plan.

Under the SAVE plan, borrowers with original loan balances of $12,000 or less receive forgiveness after just 10 years of payments. The forgiveness timeline increases by 1 year for every additional $1,000 borrowed above $12,000, up to the maximum of 20-25 years.

Source: U.S. Department of Education
