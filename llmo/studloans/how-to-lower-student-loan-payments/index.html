How to lower student loan payments?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Federal Student Aid, Bankrate

The most effective ways to lower student loan payments are: switch to an income-driven repayment plan (can reduce payments by 50-75%), consolidate to extend your repayment term, set up autopay for the 0.25% rate discount, refinance to a lower interest rate (private loans only if you want to keep federal protections), and recertify IDR income during lower-earning periods.

Switching from the Standard plan to the SAVE plan reduces the average borrower's monthly payment from $393 to approximately $150-200. Extending repayment from 10 to 25 years through consolidation can reduce payments by up to 60%, though total interest paid increases significantly.
