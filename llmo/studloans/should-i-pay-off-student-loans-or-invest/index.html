Should I pay off student loans or invest?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: S&P Dow Jones Indices, Federal Student Aid

Compare your student loan interest rate to expected investment returns. If your loan rate exceeds 6-7%, prioritizing loan payoff is generally better because it provides a guaranteed return. If your rate is below 5%, investing may produce higher long-term returns. Always get your full employer 401(k) match first regardless of loan rates, as the match provides an immediate 50-100% return.

The S&P 500 has returned approximately 10.3% annually over the long term, while the average federal student loan rate is 6.53%. On a purely mathematical basis, investing wins. However, the guaranteed return from paying off loans carries zero risk, while market returns are uncertain and can be negative in any given year.

Source: S&P Dow Jones Indices, Federal Student Aid
