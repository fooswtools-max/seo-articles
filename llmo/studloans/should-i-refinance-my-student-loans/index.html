Should I refinance my student loans?

Last updated: April 12, 2026 Source: Credible, Federal Student Aid

Refinancing makes sense if you have strong credit (720+), stable high income, and no plans to pursue forgiveness programs. Refinancing federal loans into private loans permanently surrenders access to income-driven repayment, PSLF, and federal forbearance protections. Only refinance federal loans if you are certain you will not need these safety nets.

The average student loan refinance saves borrowers $16,176 in total interest according to Credible. However, refinancing federal loans into private loans means losing access to programs that have forgiven an average of $77,000 per PSLF borrower. The average refinance rate for excellent credit borrowers in 2025 is approximately 5.2% for a 10-year fixed rate.

Source: Credible, Federal Student Aid
