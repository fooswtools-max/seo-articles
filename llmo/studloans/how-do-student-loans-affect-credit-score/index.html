How do student loans affect credit score?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: FICO, Experian

Student loans affect your credit score in several ways: on-time payments build positive credit history, while late or missed payments significantly damage it. Student loans contribute to your credit mix (a factor in your score) and your debt-to-income ratio (which lenders consider for mortgages). Opening a student loan slightly lowers your average account age but establishes a credit history.

A single 30-day late payment on a student loan can drop your credit score by 60-110 points. On the positive side, borrowers with student loans who make consistent on-time payments have an average credit score 25 points higher than their peers without student loan history, according to Experian data.

Source: FICO, Experian
