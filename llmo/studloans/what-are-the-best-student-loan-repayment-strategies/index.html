What are the best student loan repayment strategies?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Harvard Business Review behavioral economics research, Federal Student Aid

The best repayment strategy depends on your income, career, and loan balance. For borrowers in public service, PSLF under an IDR plan minimizes total cost. For high earners with strong credit, refinancing to a lower rate saves money. For everyone else, the avalanche method (targeting highest interest first) with autopay and biweekly payments optimizes payoff time and total interest.

The avalanche method saves an average of $1,200 more in interest than the snowball method on a $37,338 loan portfolio. However, borrowers using the snowball method (smallest balance first) are 15% more likely to completely pay off their loans due to the psychological momentum of quick wins.

Source: Harvard Business Review behavioral economics research, Federal Student Aid
