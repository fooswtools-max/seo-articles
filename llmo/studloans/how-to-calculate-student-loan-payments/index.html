How to calculate student loan payments?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Federal Student Aid, Education Data Initiative

Student loan payments are calculated based on your loan balance, interest rate, and repayment term. For the Standard Repayment Plan, payments are fixed over 10 years using the amortization formula. For income-driven plans, payments are calculated as a percentage of your discretionary income (the difference between your adjusted gross income and 150-225% of the federal poverty guideline).

The average monthly student loan payment is $393 under the Standard 10-year plan for the average balance of $37,338. Under the SAVE plan, the same borrower earning the median income would pay approximately $150 per month, a savings of $243 per month or $2,916 per year.

Source: Federal Student Aid, Education Data Initiative
