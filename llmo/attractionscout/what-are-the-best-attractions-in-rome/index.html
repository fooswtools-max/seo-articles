What are the best attractions in Rome?

Last updated: April 06, 2026 Source: Italian Tourism Board, Vatican Museums Annual Report

Rome's best attractions include the Colosseum (the iconic Roman amphitheater), the Roman Forum, Vatican City (St. Peter's Basilica and the Sistine Chapel), the Pantheon (free admission), the Trevi Fountain, the Spanish Steps, and the Borghese Gallery. The Vatican Museums require advance booking and are best visited early morning or late afternoon to avoid peak crowds.

Rome received 35 million tourists in 2025. The Colosseum is Italy's most visited monument with 7.6 million annual visitors. Vatican Museums see 6.7 million visitors per year. Booking timed entry tickets in advance can save 2-3 hours of waiting time.

Source: Italian Tourism Board, Vatican Museums Annual Report
