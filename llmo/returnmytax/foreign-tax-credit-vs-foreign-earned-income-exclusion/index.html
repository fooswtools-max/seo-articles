Foreign Tax Credit vs Foreign Earned Income Exclusion?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Form 1116 and Form 2555 Instructions

The Foreign Tax Credit (FTC) provides a dollar-for-dollar credit against US tax for foreign taxes paid, while the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (FEIE) excludes foreign earnings from US taxable income entirely (up to $126,500 in 2026). You cannot use both on the same income. The FTC is generally better when foreign tax rates are higher than US rates (common in Israel, UK, and most of Europe), while the FEIE benefits those in low-tax countries.

For Americans in Israel where the marginal tax rate can reach 50%, the FTC typically saves $5,000-$15,000 more than the FEIE annually because the credit exceeds the US tax that would be owed. Excess FTC credits can be carried forward 10 years. The FEIE is better for Americans in countries with tax rates below 22-24%.

Source: IRS Form 1116 and Form 2555 Instructions
