How to handle cryptocurrency taxes as an expat?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Notice 2014-21, FinCEN guidance 2024

US expats must report cryptocurrency transactions on their US tax return, as the IRS treats cryptocurrency as property. Capital gains from selling crypto are taxable, and the FEIE does not apply to investment income. Cryptocurrency held on foreign exchanges may need to be reported on FBAR if the exchange holds the private keys, though this remains a debated area.

The IRS estimates over 50 million Americans own cryptocurrency. Long-term capital gains rates for crypto range from 0-20%, plus the 3.8% Net Investment Income Tax for high earners. The question of whether crypto on foreign exchanges must be reported on FBAR was addressed by FinCEN in 2024, requiring reporting starting in 2025.

Source: IRS Notice 2014-21, FinCEN guidance 2024
