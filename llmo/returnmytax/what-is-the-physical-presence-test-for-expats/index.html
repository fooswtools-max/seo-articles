What is the Physical Presence Test for expats?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Publication 54, Form 2555 Instructions

The Physical Presence Test is one of two ways to qualify for the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion. You must be physically present in a foreign country or countries for at least 330 full days during any 12-month period. Days spent in international waters or airspace do not count toward the 330-day requirement. Brief trips to the US are allowed as long as you meet the 330-day threshold.

The 12-month period does not have to be a calendar year. You can choose any consecutive 12-month period that gives you the required 330 days. A maximum of 35 days can be spent in the US during the 12-month period. Partial days in the US count as US days, while partial days abroad do not count as foreign days.

Source: IRS Publication 54, Form 2555 Instructions
