Do US expats pay double tax?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS, Tax Foundation

No, US expats generally do not pay double tax. The US tax system provides two main mechanisms to prevent double taxation: the Foreign Tax Credit (which offsets US tax dollar-for-dollar for foreign taxes paid) and the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (which excludes up to $126,500 from US taxable income). Tax treaties between the US and other countries provide additional protection.

For Americans in high-tax countries like Israel, the UK, and Germany, the Foreign Tax Credit typically eliminates all or nearly all US tax liability because foreign tax rates are higher. An estimated 70% of US expats who file correctly owe zero additional US tax. However, self-employment tax and certain types of investment income may still be owed.
