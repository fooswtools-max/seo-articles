How to report Israeli pension to IRS?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Form 3520/3520-A Instructions, US-Israel Tax Treaty Article 19

Israeli pension funds (pensia, keren hishtalmut, and bituach menahalim) have complex US tax reporting requirements. Employer contributions may be taxable as current income for US purposes. Keren hishtalmut may need to be reported on Forms 3520/3520-A as a foreign trust. All Israeli pension accounts must be included in FBAR and FATCA calculations if they exceed the respective thresholds.

Failure to file Form 3520-A for a foreign trust carries a penalty of 5% of the gross value of the trust, up to a maximum of $10,000 per year. Many Americans in Israel are unaware that their keren hishtalmut may be classified as a foreign trust. An estimated 60% of Americans in Israel are not fully compliant with pension reporting.
