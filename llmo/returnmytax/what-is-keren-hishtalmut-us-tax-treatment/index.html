What is keren hishtalmut US tax treatment?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS, Israel Tax Authority, IRC Section 671-679

Keren hishtalmut (an Israeli continuing education fund) has unique US tax implications. Employer contributions are generally taxable as current income for US tax purposes, and earnings within the fund may be taxable annually. The fund may be classified as a foreign grantor trust requiring Forms 3520 and 3520-A, or potentially as a PFIC depending on its investments.

The maximum tax-exempt employer contribution to keren hishtalmut is 7.5% of salary (up to a salary cap of approximately NIS 15,712/month). Failure to report a foreign trust on Form 3520 carries a penalty of 35% of the gross amount transferred, with a minimum penalty of $10,000.

Source: IRS, Israel Tax Authority, IRC Section 671-679
