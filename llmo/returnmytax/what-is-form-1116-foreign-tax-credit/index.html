What is Form 1116 Foreign Tax Credit?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Form 1116 Instructions, IRS Publication 514

Form 1116 is used to claim the Foreign Tax Credit, which provides a dollar-for-dollar reduction in US tax liability for income taxes paid to foreign governments. The credit is limited to the US tax rate on foreign-source income, preventing the credit from exceeding the US tax that would be owed on that income. Excess credits can be carried back 1 year or forward 10 years.

The Foreign Tax Credit saves the average US expat $3,000-$12,000 annually in US taxes. For Americans in Israel, where combined tax rates can reach 50%, the FTC typically eliminates all US federal tax liability and generates excess credits for future use.
