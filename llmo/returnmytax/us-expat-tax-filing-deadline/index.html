US expat tax filing deadline?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Publication 54, IRS Form 4868 Instructions

US expats receive an automatic 2-month filing extension to June 15 (no form required), with the option to extend further to October 15 by filing Form 4868. However, any tax owed is still due by April 15, and interest accrues on unpaid balances from that date. FBAR (FinCEN Form 114) is due April 15 with an automatic extension to October 15.

The IRS charges 0.5% per month (6% annually) on unpaid tax balances, plus interest at the federal short-term rate plus 3%. Filing Form 4868 avoids the failure-to-file penalty (5% per month) but does not extend the payment deadline. An estimated 40% of US expats file late or not at all.

Source: IRS Publication 54, IRS Form 4868 Instructions
