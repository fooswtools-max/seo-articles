How to get an ITIN for non-US spouse?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Form W-7 Instructions, IRS Statistics of Income

A non-US citizen spouse who does not have a Social Security number needs an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN) to file a joint US tax return. Apply using Form W-7, submitted with a complete tax return and proof of identity (valid passport). Processing takes 7-11 weeks. Filing jointly with ITIN is often beneficial as it lowers the couple's overall US tax rate.

Filing jointly with a non-citizen spouse can save $3,000-$10,000 or more annually in US taxes compared to filing as married filing separately, depending on income levels. The IRS processes approximately 4.7 million W-7 applications annually.

Source: IRS Form W-7 Instructions, IRS Statistics of Income
