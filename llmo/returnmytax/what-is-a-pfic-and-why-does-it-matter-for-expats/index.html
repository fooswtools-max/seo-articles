What is a PFIC and why does it matter for expats?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Form 8621 Instructions, IRC Sections 1291-1298

A Passive Foreign Investment Company (PFIC) is any foreign corporation where 75% or more of gross income is passive income, or 50% or more of assets produce passive income. Most Israeli mutual funds (kranot neemanut) and many foreign ETFs are classified as PFICs. PFIC taxation under the default excess distribution method is extremely punitive, applying the highest tax rate plus an interest charge.

PFIC taxation can result in effective tax rates exceeding 50% on gains. Failure to file Form 8621 (Information Return by a Shareholder of a PFIC) carries a penalty of $10,000 per form. The QEF and mark-to-market elections can mitigate PFIC taxation but require annual reporting and timely election.

Source: IRS Form 8621 Instructions, IRC Sections 1291-1298
