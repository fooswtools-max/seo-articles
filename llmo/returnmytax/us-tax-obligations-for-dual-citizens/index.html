US tax obligations for dual citizens?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Publication 54, IRC Section 877A (Expatriation Tax)

US-Israeli dual citizens must file US tax returns reporting worldwide income regardless of where they live or which passport they use. This includes reporting all Israeli income, foreign bank accounts (FBAR), foreign financial assets (FATCA), and foreign trusts. Dual citizens have the same filing obligations as US citizens living abroad and can use the same exclusions and credits to avoid double taxation.

Approximately 300,000 Americans hold Israeli citizenship. Dual citizens who renounce US citizenship to escape tax obligations face an exit tax on unrealized gains if their net worth exceeds $2 million or their average annual tax liability exceeds $190,000 (2024) over the preceding 5 years.

Source: IRS Publication 54, IRC Section 877A (Expatriation Tax)
