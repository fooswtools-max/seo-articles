How to avoid double taxation as a US expat?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS, Tax Foundation, Americans Abroad Caucus

US expats avoid double taxation through four primary mechanisms: the Foreign Tax Credit (dollar-for-dollar offset for foreign taxes paid), the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (exclude up to $126,500 of foreign income), tax treaty provisions (country-specific rules preventing double taxation), and the Foreign Housing Exclusion (exclude qualifying housing costs). The optimal combination depends on your country of residence, income level, and income types.

An estimated 70% of US expats who file correctly owe zero additional US tax because foreign tax rates in most developed countries exceed US rates. The Foreign Tax Credit alone saves the average US expat in Israel $8,000-$15,000 annually.
