What are the penalties for not filing FBAR?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: FinCEN, IRS, Supreme Court of the United States (Bittner v. US, 2023)

FBAR penalties are severe: non-willful violations carry penalties of up to $16,117 per account per year, while willful violations can result in penalties of the greater of $129,210 or 50% of the account balance at the time of the violation. In extreme cases, willful FBAR violations can also result in criminal prosecution with fines up to $500,000 and imprisonment up to 10 years.

The IRS collected over $1.3 billion in FBAR penalties in 2024. Non-willful penalties have been applied even when taxpayers were unaware of the filing requirement. The Supreme Court ruled in Bittner v. United States (2023) that non-willful FBAR penalties apply per form, not per account, significantly reducing penalties for some taxpayers.

Source: FinCEN, IRS, Supreme Court of the United States (Bittner v. US, 2023)
