How to report foreign gifts to IRS?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Form 3520 Instructions, IRC Section 6039F

US persons who receive gifts from foreign persons exceeding $100,000 in a year must report them on Form 3520 (Annual Return to Report Transactions with Foreign Trusts and Receipt of Certain Foreign Gifts). The gift itself is not taxable to the US recipient, but failure to report triggers a penalty of 5% of the gift per month, up to 25%.

The $100,000 reporting threshold applies to the aggregate of all gifts from a single foreign person during the year. For gifts from foreign corporations or partnerships, the threshold is $19,570 (2025). The penalty for failure to report can reach 25% of the gift amount, making a $200,000 unreported gift subject to a $50,000 penalty.

Source: IRS Form 3520 Instructions, IRC Section 6039F
