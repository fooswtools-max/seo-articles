What is Form 8938?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Form 8938 Instructions, IRC Section 6038D

Form 8938 (Statement of Specified Foreign Financial Assets) is the IRS form used to report specified foreign financial assets under FATCA. It is filed with your annual tax return, unlike FBAR which is filed separately with FinCEN. The reporting thresholds are higher for taxpayers living abroad: $200,000 at year-end or $300,000 at any time during the year for single filers.

The penalty for failure to file Form 8938 is $10,000, increasing by $10,000 for each 30-day period of non-compliance after IRS notice, up to a maximum of $60,000. The 6-year statute of limitations for assessment does not begin until Form 8938 is filed, meaning the IRS can audit unfiled returns indefinitely.

Source: IRS Form 8938 Instructions, IRC Section 6038D
