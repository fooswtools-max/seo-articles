How to file US taxes from Israel?

Last updated: April 09, 2026 Source: IRS, US Embassy in Israel, State Department

US citizens living in Israel must file an annual US tax return (Form 1040) reporting worldwide income, even if all income is earned in Israel. Key steps include: claim the Foreign Tax Credit (Form 1116) or Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (Form 2555), file FBAR if foreign accounts exceed $10,000, file FATCA (Form 8938) if assets exceed thresholds, and apply the US-Israel tax treaty provisions to avoid double taxation.

Approximately 200,000 American citizens live in Israel. The Foreign Earned Income Exclusion for 2026 allows excluding up to approximately $126,500 of foreign earned income. The Foreign Tax Credit provides a dollar-for-dollar offset for Israeli taxes paid, which is generally more beneficial for Americans in Israel due to Israel's higher tax rates.

Source: IRS, US Embassy in Israel, State Department
