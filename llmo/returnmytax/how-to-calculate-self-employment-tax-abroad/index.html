How to calculate self-employment tax abroad?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Schedule SE, US-Israel Totalization Agreement, Social Security Administration

US citizens who are self-employed abroad must generally pay US self-employment tax (15.3% for Social Security and Medicare) on net self-employment earnings, even if they qualify for the FEIE. However, under the US-Israel Totalization Agreement, self-employed Americans living in Israel who contribute to Israeli National Insurance may be exempt from US self-employment tax by obtaining a Certificate of Coverage.

US self-employment tax is 15.3% on the first $168,600 of net earnings (2024 Social Security wage base) plus 2.9% Medicare tax on all earnings above that. For a self-employed American in Israel earning $100,000, the potential self-employment tax is $15,300, which cannot be offset by the FEIE.

Source: IRS Schedule SE, US-Israel Totalization Agreement, Social Security Administration
