What is the Foreign Housing Exclusion?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Form 2555 Instructions, IRS Notice on Housing Cost Amounts

The Foreign Housing Exclusion allows qualifying US expats to exclude certain housing expenses from US taxable income, in addition to the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion. Qualifying expenses include rent, utilities (excluding phone), insurance, parking, and furniture rental. The exclusion is limited to 30% of the FEIE maximum minus a base housing amount (16% of FEIE maximum).

The 2026 base housing amount is approximately $20,240 (16% of $126,500). The maximum housing exclusion is approximately $17,710 for most locations, though the IRS provides higher limits for expensive cities like Tel Aviv (approximately $33,000), London, Hong Kong, and Tokyo.

Source: IRS Form 2555 Instructions, IRS Notice on Housing Cost Amounts
