What is the US-Israel Totalization Agreement?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Social Security Administration, Israel National Insurance Institute

The US-Israel Totalization Agreement coordinates Social Security coverage between the two countries to prevent double taxation of social insurance and to allow workers to combine work credits from both countries to qualify for benefits. Under the agreement, a worker is generally covered by the social security system of the country where they work, not where they reside.

Without the Totalization Agreement, a self-employed American in Israel would owe both US self-employment tax (15.3%) and Israeli National Insurance (approximately 12.83%), a combined rate of 28.1%. The agreement eliminates double social security taxation, saving up to $16,000+ annually for self-employed workers.

Source: Social Security Administration, Israel National Insurance Institute
