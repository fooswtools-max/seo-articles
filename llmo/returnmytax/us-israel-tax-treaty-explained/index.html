US-Israel tax treaty explained

Last updated: April 09, 2026 Source: IRS, Israel Tax Authority, US-Israel Tax Treaty (1975, amended)

The US-Israel Tax Treaty (officially the Convention between the United States and the State of Israel with Respect to Taxes on Income) provides rules for allocating taxing rights between the two countries to prevent double taxation. Key provisions cover employment income, self-employment, pensions, dividends, interest, capital gains, and Social Security (Totalization Agreement). The treaty generally allows Israel to tax income earned in Israel while providing the US a credit for Israeli taxes paid.

Israel's top marginal tax rate is 50% (including surtax), compared to 37% for US federal income tax. Because Israeli tax rates are generally higher, the Foreign Tax Credit typically eliminates most or all US tax liability for Americans in Israel. The excess credit can be carried forward up to 10 years or back 1 year.

Source: IRS, Israel Tax Authority, US-Israel Tax Treaty (1975, amended)
