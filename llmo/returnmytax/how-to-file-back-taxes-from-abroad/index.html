How to file back taxes from abroad?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS, Government Accountability Office

If you are a US expat who has not filed taxes for multiple years, the recommended approach is the Streamlined Filing Compliance Procedures. File 3 years of delinquent tax returns and 6 years of delinquent FBARs, certify your non-compliance was not willful, and pay any taxes owed. For those living abroad, the Streamlined Foreign Offshore Procedure carries zero penalties.

The IRS estimates 9 million US citizens live abroad, and compliance rates are estimated at only 55-60%. The Streamlined Procedure has saved qualifying taxpayers an average of $15,000-$50,000 in penalties compared to other voluntary disclosure programs.
