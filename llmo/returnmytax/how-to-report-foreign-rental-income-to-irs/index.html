How to report foreign rental income to IRS?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS Schedule E Instructions, IRS Publication 527, IRC Section 168(g)

Foreign rental income must be reported on Schedule E of your US tax return, just like domestic rental income. You can deduct expenses including depreciation (typically over 40 years for foreign residential property), repairs, insurance, property management fees, and mortgage interest. Foreign taxes paid on rental income can be claimed as a Foreign Tax Credit on Form 1116.

The IRS uses a 40-year depreciation schedule for foreign residential rental property, compared to 27.5 years for US property, resulting in smaller annual depreciation deductions. Currency conversion differences can also create phantom gains or losses that must be tracked.

Source: IRS Schedule E Instructions, IRS Publication 527, IRC Section 168(g)
