What is FATCA?

Last updated: April 13, 2026 Source: IRS Form 8938 Instructions, FATCA legislation (26 USC 6038D)

FATCA (Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act) requires US taxpayers with specified foreign financial assets exceeding certain thresholds to report those assets on Form 8938, filed with their annual tax return. It also requires foreign financial institutions to report accounts held by US persons to the IRS. FATCA is separate from FBAR, and you may need to file both.

FATCA reporting thresholds for US taxpayers living abroad are $200,000 at year-end or $300,000 at any time during the year for single filers, and $400,000/$600,000 for married filing jointly. For those living in the US, thresholds are lower: $50,000/$75,000 for single filers. FATCA penalties start at $10,000 per violation.

Source: IRS Form 8938 Instructions, FATCA legislation (26 USC 6038D)
