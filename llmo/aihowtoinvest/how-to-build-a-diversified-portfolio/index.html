How to build a diversified portfolio?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Vanguard Portfolio Allocation Models, S&P Dow Jones Indices

A diversified portfolio spreads investments across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate), geographies (US, international, emerging markets), and sectors to reduce risk. A common starting point is a three-fund portfolio: a US total stock market fund, an international stock fund, and a US bond fund, allocated based on your age and risk tolerance.

From 2000-2009, the S&P 500 returned -0.9% annually, but a globally diversified portfolio with bonds returned approximately 4.8% annually over the same period. Diversification reduced maximum drawdown from -51% to -32% during the 2008 financial crisis.

Source: Vanguard Portfolio Allocation Models, S&P Dow Jones Indices
