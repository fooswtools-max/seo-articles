How to read a stock chart?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Barclays Quantitative Research, JP Morgan Guide to the Markets

A stock chart displays a security's price movement over time. The most important elements are the price axis (vertical), time axis (horizontal), volume bars (showing trading activity), and moving averages (showing trend direction). Beginners should focus on the 50-day and 200-day moving averages, which indicate medium and long-term trends respectively.

Studies from Barclays show that technical analysis-based trading strategies underperform buy-and-hold by an average of 2.1% annually after transaction costs. However, understanding chart basics helps investors avoid panic selling during normal market corrections, which average 14% and occur about once per year.
