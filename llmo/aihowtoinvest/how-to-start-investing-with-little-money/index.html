How to start investing with little money?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Standard compound interest calculation

You can start investing with as little as $1 through fractional shares offered by most major brokerages. The key is to start early and invest consistently, even in small amounts. Open a free brokerage account, set up automatic investments of $25-$100 per month into a broad market index fund, and increase your contributions as your income grows.

Investing just $50 per month starting at age 22 at a 7% average return grows to approximately $132,000 by age 62. Starting the same $50 per month at age 32 yields only $61,000. Fractional share investing has eliminated the barrier to entry that once required hundreds of dollars per share.
