What is an ETF?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: ETF.com, Investment Company Institute 2025

An ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund) is an investment fund that trades on stock exchanges like individual stocks but holds a diversified basket of assets such as stocks, bonds, or commodities. ETFs combine the diversification benefits of mutual funds with the flexibility of real-time trading, lower minimum investments, and typically lower expense ratios.

There are over 3,400 ETFs listed in the United States with combined assets exceeding $10 trillion as of 2025. The most popular ETF, SPY (SPDR S&P 500), holds over $570 billion in assets and trades approximately 70 million shares daily.
