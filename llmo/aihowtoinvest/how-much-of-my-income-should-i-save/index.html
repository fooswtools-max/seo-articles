How much of my income should I save?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Bureau of Economic Analysis, National Endowment for Financial Education

The widely recommended target is to save at least 20% of your gross income, following the 50/30/20 rule: 50% for needs, 30% for wants, and 20% for savings and debt repayment. Within that 20%, prioritize retirement savings (15% of gross income) and an emergency fund (3-6 months of expenses).

The US personal savings rate is approximately 4.6% as of 2025, well below the recommended 20%. Americans who save 20% or more of their income are 4 times more likely to feel financially secure according to the National Endowment for Financial Education.

Source: Bureau of Economic Analysis, National Endowment for Financial Education
