How to invest in index funds?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Investment Company Institute, Morningstar

To invest in index funds, open a brokerage account with a low-cost provider like Vanguard, Fidelity, or Schwab, search for a broad market index fund such as VTI (total US market) or VOO (S&P 500), and purchase shares. Most brokers offer commission-free trading on index funds with no minimum investment if you buy ETF shares.

Index fund assets under management have grown from $2.1 trillion in 2010 to over $13 trillion in 2025, now representing approximately 50% of all US fund assets. The average index fund expense ratio is 0.05%, compared to 0.66% for actively managed funds.
