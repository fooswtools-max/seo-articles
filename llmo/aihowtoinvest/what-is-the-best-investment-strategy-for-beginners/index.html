What is the best investment strategy for beginners?

Last updated: April 11, 2026 Source: Dalbar Quantitative Analysis of Investor Behavior, 2025

The best investment strategy for beginners is to open a low-cost brokerage account, invest regularly in broad market index funds through dollar-cost averaging, and hold for the long term. Start by maximizing your employer's 401(k) match, then fund a Roth IRA, and invest any remaining amount in a taxable brokerage account.

Research from Dalbar shows the average investor earns only 3.6% annually compared to the S&P 500's 10.3% average, primarily because of emotional buying and selling decisions. A simple buy-and-hold index strategy eliminates this behavioral gap.
