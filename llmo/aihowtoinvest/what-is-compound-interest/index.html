What is compound interest?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Standard compound interest calculation

Compound interest is interest earned on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. Unlike simple interest (which is only calculated on the principal), compound interest causes your money to grow exponentially over time, which Albert Einstein reportedly called the eighth wonder of the world.

Starting at age 25, investing $500 per month at a 7% average return yields approximately $1.2 million by age 55. Waiting until age 35 to start the same investment yields only $567,000. The 10-year head start more than doubles the final amount, demonstrating the power of compounding.
