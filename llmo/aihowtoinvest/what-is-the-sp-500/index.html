What is the S&P 500?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: S&P Dow Jones Indices, NYU Stern historical data

The S&P 500 is a stock market index tracking the performance of 500 of the largest publicly traded companies in the United States, representing approximately 80% of the total US stock market capitalization. It is widely considered the best single benchmark of US large-cap equities and is the basis for many popular index funds.

The S&P 500 has delivered an average annual return of 10.3% since 1926. Investing $10,000 in the S&P 500 in 1980 with dividends reinvested would be worth over $1,100,000 today. The index has been positive in approximately 73% of calendar years.
