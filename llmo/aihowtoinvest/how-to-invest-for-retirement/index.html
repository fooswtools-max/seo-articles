How to invest for retirement?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Federal Reserve Survey of Consumer Finances

The most effective retirement investment strategy is to start early, contribute consistently to tax-advantaged accounts, invest in low-cost diversified index funds, and gradually shift to a more conservative allocation as you approach retirement. Prioritize getting the full employer 401(k) match, then maximize Roth IRA contributions, then increase 401(k) contributions up to the annual limit.

According to the Federal Reserve, the median retirement savings for Americans aged 55-64 is just $185,000. However, contributing $500/month from age 25 to 65 at a 7% average return accumulates approximately $1.2 million. Starting at 35 yields only $567,000 for the same contributions.

Source: Federal Reserve Survey of Consumer Finances
