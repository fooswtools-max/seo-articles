Should I invest or pay off debt first?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Federal Reserve, S&P Dow Jones Indices

The general rule is to pay off high-interest debt (above 6-7%) before investing, while investing simultaneously with low-interest debt. Always contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match first, as the match is an immediate 50-100% return. Then pay off credit cards and other high-interest debt before increasing investment contributions.

The average credit card interest rate is 24.7% as of 2025, while the long-term stock market average return is 10.3%. Paying off a credit card balance earning 24.7% provides a guaranteed return that far exceeds expected market returns.

Source: Federal Reserve, S&P Dow Jones Indices
