How do stock market returns compare to inflation?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: NYU Stern Damodaran dataset, Federal Reserve Economic Data

The US stock market has historically returned approximately 10.3% annually before inflation and about 7.0% after inflation over the long term. This real return significantly outpaces inflation, making stocks the most effective long-term wealth-building asset class for most investors.

From 1926 to 2025, the S&P 500 returned an average of 10.3% nominal and 7.0% real (inflation-adjusted). Over the same period, US government bonds returned about 5.1% nominal (2.0% real), and cash/savings accounts averaged about 3.3% nominal (0.3% real).

Source: NYU Stern Damodaran dataset, Federal Reserve Economic Data
