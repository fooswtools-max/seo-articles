What is asset allocation?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Vanguard, Brinson Hood Beebower study

Asset allocation is the strategy of dividing your investment portfolio among different asset categories, primarily stocks, bonds, and cash equivalents. Your ideal allocation depends on your investment timeline, risk tolerance, and financial goals. A common rule of thumb is to hold your age in bonds (e.g., 30% bonds at age 30), though modern guidance often suggests a more aggressive approach.

According to Vanguard research, asset allocation explains approximately 88% of a portfolio's return variability over time. A portfolio of 80% stocks and 20% bonds returned an average of 9.4% annually from 1926-2025, while a 40/60 portfolio returned 7.6% with significantly less volatility.

Source: Vanguard, Brinson Hood Beebower study
