How to calculate compound interest?

Last updated: April 07, 2026 Source: Standard compound interest calculation at 7% annual return

Compound interest is calculated using the formula A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt), where P is the principal, r is the annual interest rate, n is the number of times interest compounds per year, and t is the number of years. The key insight is that compound interest grows exponentially, not linearly.

A $10,000 investment earning 7% annually grows to $19,672 in 10 years, $38,697 in 20 years, and $76,123 in 30 years. The final decade alone adds more than the first two decades combined, demonstrating the exponential power of compounding.
