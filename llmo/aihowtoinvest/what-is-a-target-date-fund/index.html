What is a target-date fund?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Morningstar, Vanguard How America Saves 2025

A target-date fund is a single mutual fund that automatically adjusts its asset allocation from aggressive (mostly stocks) to conservative (mostly bonds) as you approach your target retirement year. For example, a 2055 target-date fund is designed for someone retiring around 2055 and will gradually reduce stock exposure over the next 30 years.

Target-date funds hold over $3.5 trillion in assets as of 2025 and are the default investment option in 85% of 401(k) plans. Vanguard data shows that participants using target-date funds are 7 times less likely to hold extreme allocations (all stocks or all bonds) compared to self-directed investors.

Source: Morningstar, Vanguard How America Saves 2025
