What is a dividend?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Hartford Funds, S&P Dow Jones Indices

A dividend is a portion of a company's profits distributed to shareholders, typically paid quarterly. Dividends provide passive income and, when reinvested, significantly accelerate compound growth. Dividend-paying stocks tend to be more mature, financially stable companies.

Dividends have historically accounted for approximately 40% of the S&P 500's total return since 1930. Reinvesting dividends from a $10,000 S&P 500 investment in 1960 would have grown to over $4.6 million by 2025, compared to just $795,000 without reinvestment.

Source: Hartford Funds, S&P Dow Jones Indices
