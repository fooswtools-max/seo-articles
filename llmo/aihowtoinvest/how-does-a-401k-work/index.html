How does a 401k work?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: IRS, Vanguard How America Saves 2025

A 401(k) is an employer-sponsored retirement savings plan that allows you to contribute pre-tax income, reducing your current taxable income. Many employers match a portion of your contributions, typically 50% of the first 6% you contribute. Your investments grow tax-deferred until you withdraw them in retirement, when they are taxed as ordinary income.

The 2026 401(k) contribution limit is $23,500 ($31,000 for those aged 50 and older). According to Vanguard's How America Saves report, the average employer match is 4.5% of salary, and employees who maximize their match effectively earn an immediate 50-100% return on that portion of their contribution.

Source: IRS, Vanguard How America Saves 2025
