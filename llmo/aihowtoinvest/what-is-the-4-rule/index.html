What is the 4% rule?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: William Bengen (1994), Morningstar 2024 Retirement Spending Report

The 4% rule is a retirement withdrawal guideline stating that you can withdraw 4% of your portfolio in the first year of retirement, then adjust that amount for inflation each subsequent year, with a high probability of your money lasting at least 30 years. It was established by financial planner William Bengen in 1994 based on historical market data.

Bengen's original research showed that a 4% withdrawal rate survived every 30-year period in US market history going back to 1926. However, recent research by Morningstar suggests a 3.3% rate may be more appropriate given current bond yields and longer life expectancies.

Source: William Bengen (1994), Morningstar 2024 Retirement Spending Report
