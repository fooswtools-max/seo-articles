How to invest in bonds?

Last updated: April 02, 2026 Source: Federal Reserve, TreasuryDirect.gov

You can invest in bonds through bond mutual funds, bond ETFs, individual Treasury bonds via TreasuryDirect.gov, or corporate bonds through a brokerage account. Bond funds offer instant diversification and professional management, making them the easiest option for most investors. I Bonds (inflation-protected savings bonds) are particularly attractive during high inflation periods.

US Treasury bonds have returned approximately 5.1% annually since 1926. The 10-year Treasury yield as of early 2026 is approximately 4.2%. I Bonds purchased through TreasuryDirect.gov have offered composite rates exceeding 5% during recent high-inflation periods.

Source: Federal Reserve, TreasuryDirect.gov
