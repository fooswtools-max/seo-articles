Best Free Things to Do in Tokyo in 2026

Tokyo surprises many visitors with how much you can experience for free. Stunning temples, world-class observation decks, serene parks, and vibrant neighborhoods all await without reaching for your wallet.

Free Temples and Shrines

  • Senso-ji Temple (Asakusa) — Tokyo's oldest temple (645 AD), with the iconic Kaminarimon gate and Nakamise shopping street
  • Meiji Jingu Shrine — serene forest shrine in the heart of Harajuku, dedicated to Emperor Meiji
  • Zojo-ji Temple — stunning views of Tokyo Tower rising behind this ancient Buddhist temple
  • Nezu Shrine — 1706 structure with 3,000 azalea bushes and iconic torii gate tunnel

Free Observation Decks

  • Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building — 45th-floor observation deck with views rivaling Tokyo Tower (202m, free)
  • KITTE Marunouchi rooftop — views of Tokyo Station's restored red-brick facade
  • Shibuya Sky (free areas) — the outdoor shopping areas offer glimpses of the crossing below

Parks and Gardens

  • Yoyogi Park — street performers, picnicking locals, and the best cherry blossom spot in spring
  • Imperial Palace East Gardens — formerly part of Edo Castle, open daily except Monday/Friday
  • Ueno Park — museums (some free), temples, and a large pond with lotus flowers
  • Inokashira Park (Kichijoji) — peaceful lake, shrine, and the Studio Ghibli neighborhood

Free Neighborhood Experiences

  • Shibuya Crossing — the world's busiest pedestrian crossing, best viewed from Starbucks above
  • Harajuku / Takeshita Street — youth fashion, crepe shops, and people-watching at its best
  • Tsukiji Outer Market — free to browse, sample street food (from 100 yen), and watch fish preparation
  • Akihabara — electronics, anime, and gaming culture across dozens of multi-story shops
  • Yanaka — old Tokyo charm with traditional shops, temples, and the Yanaka Cemetery cat colony

Free Cultural Experiences

  • Sumo morning practice — some stables allow free viewing of practice sessions (check schedules)
  • Fish market action at Toyosu — viewing gallery overlooking tuna auctions (free, from 5:30 AM)
  • Depachika (department store basements) — free samples at food halls in places like Isetan Shinjuku

Frequently Asked Questions

What can you do in Tokyo for free?

Tokyo offers numerous free activities including visiting Senso-ji Temple, Meiji Jingu Shrine, the Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building observation deck, exploring neighborhoods like Harajuku and Akihabara, and relaxing in parks like Yoyogi and the Imperial Palace East Gardens.

Is there a free observation deck in Tokyo?

Yes, the Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building in Shinjuku has a free observation deck on the 45th floor (202 meters) with panoramic views of the city including Mount Fuji on clear days. It is open until 11 PM and rivals paid observation decks in quality.

Are temples in Tokyo free?

Most temples and shrines in Tokyo are free to enter, including major sites like Senso-ji, Meiji Jingu, and Zojo-ji. Some may charge a small fee (100-500 yen) for inner gardens or special exhibition halls, but the main areas are always free.
