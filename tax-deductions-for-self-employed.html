Tax Deductions for Self Employeds - Complete 2026 Guide

Updated March 2026 · ReturnMyTax

As a self employed, you likely have numerous tax deductions available that could save you thousands of dollars each year. This guide covers every major deduction self employed can claim on their 2026 tax returns.

Average potential savings: $5,000 - $20,000 per year

Top Tax Deductions for Self Employed

  • Self-employment tax deduction (50%)
  • SEP IRA or Solo 401(k) contributions
  • Home office deduction
  • Vehicle expenses (standard mileage or actual)
  • Health and dental insurance premiums

How Tax Deductions Work for Self Employed

Tax deductions reduce your taxable income, which lowers the amount of tax you owe. For self employed, deductions are typically claimed on Schedule C (self-employed) or as adjustments to income on Form 1040.

Special rules: Must file Schedule SE; can deduct employer-equivalent portion of self-employment tax

Detailed Deduction Breakdown

Business Expenses

Any expense that is ordinary and necessary for your work as a self employed is deductible. This includes supplies, equipment, software subscriptions, and professional services directly related to generating income.

Home Office Deduction

If you use part of your home exclusively for business, you can deduct either $5 per square foot (up to $1,500 with the simplified method) or your actual expenses proportional to your office space.

Vehicle and Mileage

For 2026, the standard mileage rate is used for business driving. Track all business miles using an app or mileage log. You can deduct either the standard mileage rate or actual vehicle expenses.

Professional Development

Courses, certifications, conferences, books, and subscriptions that maintain or improve your skills as a self employed are fully deductible.

Health Insurance

Self-employed self employed can deduct 100% of health, dental, and long-term care insurance premiums for themselves and their families, reducing adjusted gross income.

Tax Filing Tips for Self Employed

  1. Track everything — Use accounting software to categorize expenses throughout the year
  2. Keep receipts — Save receipts for all business purchases over $75
  3. Separate accounts — Use a dedicated business bank account and credit card
  4. Estimate quarterly — Pay estimated taxes quarterly to avoid penalties
  5. Consider a CPA — Professional tax preparation often pays for itself in found deductions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the self-employment tax deduction?

Self-employed individuals pay both the employer and employee portions of Social Security and Medicare taxes (15.3% total). You can deduct the employer-equivalent portion (7.65%) from your adjusted gross income on Form 1040.

What retirement plans can self-employed people use?

Self-employed individuals can contribute to a SEP IRA (up to 25% of net earnings), Solo 401(k) (up to $23,500 employee + 25% employer in 2026), or SIMPLE IRA. These contributions reduce your taxable income significantly.

How does the home office deduction work for self-employed?

You can deduct home office expenses using the simplified method ($5 per square foot, max $1,500) or the regular method (actual expenses proportional to office space). The space must be used regularly and exclusively for business.
