How long does student loan refinancing take?

Updated March 29, 2026 · StudLoans

To effectively how long does student loan refinancing take, compare all available repayment plans and forgiveness programs before making a decision.

Look Into Employer Repayment Benefits

A growing number of employers now offer student loan repayment assistance as a benefit, with some providing up to $5,250 per year in tax-free contributions toward your loans.

Check State-Level Forgiveness Programs

Many states offer additional loan forgiveness or repayment assistance for healthcare workers, teachers, lawyers, and other professionals who work in underserved areas.

Claim the Student Loan Interest Deduction

You can deduct up to $2,500 in student loan interest per year on your federal taxes, even if you take the standard deduction. This reduces your taxable income and your tax bill.

Set Up Autopay for the Rate Discount

Most federal and private loan servicers offer a 0.25% interest rate reduction when you enroll in automatic payments. This is free money that adds up significantly over years of repayment.

Explore Income-Driven Repayment Plans

Under the SAVE plan, payments can be as low as 5% of discretionary income for undergraduate loans. These plans cap your payments based on what you can afford and offer forgiveness after 20-25 years.

Review Your Plan Annually

Your income, family size, and available programs change over time. Recertify your income-driven repayment plan annually and check for new forgiveness opportunities that may apply to your situation.
