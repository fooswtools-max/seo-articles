How do I make a student loan repayment plan?

Updated April 05, 2026 · StudLoans

To effectively make a student loan repayment plan, compare all available repayment plans and forgiveness programs before making a decision.

Check Your Forgiveness Eligibility

Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) forgives your remaining balance after 120 qualifying payments while working for a government or nonprofit employer. Teacher Loan Forgiveness offers up to $17,500.

Review Your Plan Annually

Your income, family size, and available programs change over time. Recertify your income-driven repayment plan annually and check for new forgiveness opportunities that may apply to your situation.

Understand Consolidation vs Refinancing

Federal loan consolidation combines your loans into one payment with a weighted average rate. Refinancing replaces your loans with a new private loan at a potentially lower rate but different terms.

Don't Ignore Your Loans

Defaulting on federal student loans triggers severe consequences including wage garnishment, tax refund seizure, credit score damage, and loss of eligibility for future financial aid.

Claim the Student Loan Interest Deduction

You can deduct up to $2,500 in student loan interest per year on your federal taxes, even if you take the standard deduction. This reduces your taxable income and your tax bill.
