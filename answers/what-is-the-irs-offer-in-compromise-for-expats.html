File Your FBAR If Accounts Exceed $10,000

If your foreign financial accounts have a combined maximum value exceeding $10,000 at any point during the year, you must file FinCEN Form 114 (FBAR) electronically by April 15.

Use the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (FEIE)

The FEIE allows you to exclude up to approximately $126,500 (2026) of foreign earned income from US taxes. You must pass either the Bona Fide Residence or Physical Presence test to qualify.

Use the Streamlined Procedure If Behind

If you have been non-compliant but it was not willful, the Streamlined Filing Compliance Procedure lets you catch up on 3 years of tax returns and 6 years of FBARs without penalties.

Check Your State Tax Obligations

Some states like California and New York may still consider you a tax resident even after moving abroad. Research your state's rules and formally establish non-residency if possible.

Claim the Foreign Tax Credit (FTC)

If you pay income taxes to a foreign country, you can claim a dollar-for-dollar credit against your US tax liability using Form 1116. This is the primary tool for preventing double taxation.
