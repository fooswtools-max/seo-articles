What is the PSLF waiver and can I still use it?

Updated April 06, 2026 · StudLoans

The pslf waiver and can i still use it requires a clear understanding of the difference between federal and private loan rules.

Don't Ignore Your Loans

Defaulting on federal student loans triggers severe consequences including wage garnishment, tax refund seizure, credit score damage, and loss of eligibility for future financial aid.

Explore Income-Driven Repayment Plans

Under the SAVE plan, payments can be as low as 5% of discretionary income for undergraduate loans. These plans cap your payments based on what you can afford and offer forgiveness after 20-25 years.

Think Carefully Before Refinancing

Refinancing federal loans into private loans means permanently losing access to income-driven repayment, forgiveness programs, and federal forbearance protections. Only refinance if you have stable high income.

Set Up Autopay for the Rate Discount

Most federal and private loan servicers offer a 0.25% interest rate reduction when you enroll in automatic payments. This is free money that adds up significantly over years of repayment.

Check Your Forgiveness Eligibility

Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) forgives your remaining balance after 120 qualifying payments while working for a government or nonprofit employer. Teacher Loan Forgiveness offers up to $17,500.
