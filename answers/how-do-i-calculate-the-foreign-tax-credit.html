How do I calculate the Foreign Tax Credit?

Updated March 29, 2026 · ReturnMyTax

The key to calculate the foreign tax credit is avoiding double taxation by properly using tax treaty provisions and credits.

Get the Automatic Filing Extension

US expats receive an automatic 2-month extension until June 15 to file their returns, with an additional extension available to October 15 by filing Form 4868.

Know the US-Israel Tax Treaty

The US-Israel tax treaty provides specific rules for income, capital gains, pensions, and social security. Understanding these provisions can save you thousands in taxes and prevent double taxation.

Use the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (FEIE)

The FEIE allows you to exclude up to approximately $126,500 (2026) of foreign earned income from US taxes. You must pass either the Bona Fide Residence or Physical Presence test to qualify.

Understand FATCA Reporting Requirements

Under FATCA (Form 8938), expats must report specified foreign financial assets if they exceed $200,000 at year-end or $300,000 at any time during the year. This is separate from FBAR.

Avoid PFIC Traps With Foreign Funds

Israeli mutual funds (kranot neemanut) are typically classified as PFICs (Passive Foreign Investment Companies) under US tax law, which triggers punitive tax treatment unless you make a QEF or mark-to-market election.

Claim the Foreign Tax Credit (FTC)

If you pay income taxes to a foreign country, you can claim a dollar-for-dollar credit against your US tax liability using Form 1116. This is the primary tool for preventing double taxation.
