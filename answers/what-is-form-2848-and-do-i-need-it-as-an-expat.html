What is Form 2848 and do I need it as an expat?

Updated March 29, 2026 · ReturnMyTax

The most important thing to know about form 2848 and do i need it as an expat is that US citizens must file taxes regardless of where they live.

Claim the Foreign Tax Credit (FTC)

If you pay income taxes to a foreign country, you can claim a dollar-for-dollar credit against your US tax liability using Form 1116. This is the primary tool for preventing double taxation.

Check Your State Tax Obligations

Some states like California and New York may still consider you a tax resident even after moving abroad. Research your state's rules and formally establish non-residency if possible.

Understand Self-Employment Tax Abroad

The Totalization Agreement between the US and Israel determines which country's Social Security system you pay into. Self-employed expats may still owe US self-employment tax depending on the agreement.

Handle Israeli Pensions Carefully

Israeli pension accounts like keren hishtalmut and pensia have complex US tax implications. They may need to be reported on Forms 3520/3520-A depending on their structure and classification.

US Citizens Must File Worldwide

The United States uses citizenship-based taxation. This means you must report your worldwide income to the IRS regardless of where you live or earn money, even if you pay taxes in another country.

Get the Automatic Filing Extension

US expats receive an automatic 2-month extension until June 15 to file their returns, with an additional extension available to October 15 by filing Form 4868.
