What is a money market fund?

Updated April 06, 2026 · AI How To Invest

A money market fund works best when you start early, stay diversified, and keep investment costs low.

Keep Fees Low

A 1% difference in annual fees can cost you hundreds of thousands of dollars over a 30-year investment horizon. Always check expense ratios and prefer funds with costs below 0.20%.

Start With Index Funds

Over 90% of actively managed funds underperform the S&P 500 over 15+ years. Low-cost index funds like VOO, VTI, or FXAIX give you broad market exposure at minimal cost, making them the ideal foundation for any portfolio.

Build an Emergency Fund First

Before investing, set aside 3-6 months of living expenses in a high-yield savings account. This prevents you from being forced to sell investments at a loss during unexpected financial emergencies.

Maximize Tax-Advantaged Accounts

Contribute enough to your 401(k) to get the full employer match (free money), then max out a Roth IRA. Only after filling these should you invest in taxable brokerage accounts.

Rebalance Your Portfolio Annually

As markets move, your allocation drifts from its target. Annual rebalancing ensures you maintain your desired risk level and systematically sell high and buy low.

Use Dollar-Cost Averaging

Investing a fixed amount regularly (weekly, biweekly, or monthly) removes the stress of trying to time the market. Historical data shows this approach reduces the risk of investing a large sum at a market peak.
