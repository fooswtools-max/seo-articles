How do I get my student loans out of default?

Updated March 29, 2026 · StudLoans

For get my student loans out of default, the first step is reviewing your federal loan servicer dashboard and exploring income-driven repayment options.

Know Your Loan Types

Federal and private student loans have completely different rules. Federal loans offer income-driven repayment plans, forgiveness programs, and deferment options that private loans do not provide.

Check State-Level Forgiveness Programs

Many states offer additional loan forgiveness or repayment assistance for healthcare workers, teachers, lawyers, and other professionals who work in underserved areas.

Think Carefully Before Refinancing

Refinancing federal loans into private loans means permanently losing access to income-driven repayment, forgiveness programs, and federal forbearance protections. Only refinance if you have stable high income.

Check Your Forgiveness Eligibility

Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) forgives your remaining balance after 120 qualifying payments while working for a government or nonprofit employer. Teacher Loan Forgiveness offers up to $17,500.

Use the Avalanche Method to Save Money

Pay the minimum on all loans and direct extra money toward the highest interest rate loan first. This mathematically minimizes the total interest you pay over the life of your loans.
