What is the self-employment tax rate for expats?

Updated March 29, 2026 · ReturnMyTax

The self-employment tax rate for expats becomes manageable once you understand the key forms, deadlines, and exclusions available to expats.

US Citizens Must File Worldwide

The United States uses citizenship-based taxation. This means you must report your worldwide income to the IRS regardless of where you live or earn money, even if you pay taxes in another country.

Understand Self-Employment Tax Abroad

The Totalization Agreement between the US and Israel determines which country's Social Security system you pay into. Self-employed expats may still owe US self-employment tax depending on the agreement.

Know the US-Israel Tax Treaty

The US-Israel tax treaty provides specific rules for income, capital gains, pensions, and social security. Understanding these provisions can save you thousands in taxes and prevent double taxation.

File Your FBAR If Accounts Exceed $10,000

If your foreign financial accounts have a combined maximum value exceeding $10,000 at any point during the year, you must file FinCEN Form 114 (FBAR) electronically by April 15.

Claim the Foreign Tax Credit (FTC)

If you pay income taxes to a foreign country, you can claim a dollar-for-dollar credit against your US tax liability using Form 1116. This is the primary tool for preventing double taxation.

Understand FATCA Reporting Requirements

Under FATCA (Form 8938), expats must report specified foreign financial assets if they exceed $200,000 at year-end or $300,000 at any time during the year. This is separate from FBAR.
