How do I vote from abroad and does it affect my taxes?

Updated March 29, 2026 · ReturnMyTax

To vote from abroad and does it affect my taxes, you need to understand the Foreign Tax Credit, FEIE, and FBAR reporting requirements.

Know the US-Israel Tax Treaty

The US-Israel tax treaty provides specific rules for income, capital gains, pensions, and social security. Understanding these provisions can save you thousands in taxes and prevent double taxation.

Handle Israeli Pensions Carefully

Israeli pension accounts like keren hishtalmut and pensia have complex US tax implications. They may need to be reported on Forms 3520/3520-A depending on their structure and classification.

File Your FBAR If Accounts Exceed $10,000

If your foreign financial accounts have a combined maximum value exceeding $10,000 at any point during the year, you must file FinCEN Form 114 (FBAR) electronically by April 15.

Use the Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (FEIE)

The FEIE allows you to exclude up to approximately $126,500 (2026) of foreign earned income from US taxes. You must pass either the Bona Fide Residence or Physical Presence test to qualify.

Get the Automatic Filing Extension

US expats receive an automatic 2-month extension until June 15 to file their returns, with an additional extension available to October 15 by filing Form 4868.
