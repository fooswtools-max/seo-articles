Can I Deduct Property Taxes on My Taxes? 2026 Guide

Updated March 2026 · ReturnMyTax

Short answer: Yes. Property Taxes expenses can be tax-deductible if you meet certain requirements. This guide explains exactly who qualifies, how much you can deduct, and how to claim property taxes on your 2026 tax return.

Can You Deduct Property Taxes?

Yes, property taxes expenses are generally tax-deductible for qualifying taxpayers. The IRS allows this deduction for self-employed individuals and some employees.

Who Qualifies?

Self-employed individuals and some employees

  • Self-employed individuals (Schedule C filers)
  • Independent contractors and freelancers
  • Small business owners
  • W-2 employees may qualify in certain states with employee expense reimbursement laws

How Much Can You Deduct?

Maximum deduction: Varies based on circumstances

Method: Actual expenses or simplified method

The amount you can deduct depends on how much of the expense is business-related. Only the business-use portion is deductible. You must be able to substantiate your claim with documentation.

How to Claim This Deduction

  1. Determine eligibility — Confirm you qualify based on your employment status
  2. Calculate the business percentage — Determine what portion is for business use
  3. Gather documentation — Collect bills, receipts, and usage logs
  4. Report on your tax return — Claim on Schedule C (self-employed) or applicable form
  5. Keep records — Retain documentation for at least 3 years in case of audit

Documentation Requirements

The IRS may request proof of your deduction. Keep these records:

  • Monthly bills and statements
  • Business use log or percentage calculation
  • Receipts for any related purchases
  • Notes explaining the business purpose

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Deducting 100% without business use documentation — Only the business portion qualifies
  • Claiming as a W-2 employee on federal taxes — Unreimbursed employee expenses are not deductible federally since 2017
  • Not keeping adequate records — Without documentation, the IRS can disallow the deduction
  • Mixing personal and business expenses — Keep clear separation for audit protection
