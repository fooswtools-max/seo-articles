Best Free Things to Do in Paris (2026)

Best Free Things to Do in Paris in 2026

Paris has a well-deserved reputation as an expensive city, but some of its finest experiences cost nothing. From world-class museums with free admission days to stunning architecture you can admire from the street, here are the best free things to do in Paris.

Free Museums and Galleries

  • Musee d'Art Moderne de Paris — permanent collection is always free, featuring Matisse, Picasso, and Modigliani
  • Petit Palais — free permanent collection including Rembrandt, Monet, and Cezanne in a stunning Beaux-Arts building
  • Musee Carnavalet — the history of Paris from prehistoric times, beautifully renovated in 2021
  • First Sunday of the month — the Louvre, Musee d'Orsay, and Centre Pompidou offer free entry (arrive early)
  • Under 26 and EU resident? — most national museums are free year-round

Parks and Gardens

  • Luxembourg Gardens — 23 hectares of manicured gardens, fountains, and free chair seating (the quintessential Paris experience)
  • Tuileries Garden — from the Louvre to Place de la Concorde, perfect for a long stroll
  • Parc des Buttes-Chaumont — dramatic cliffs, waterfalls, and a temple with panoramic views (less touristy)
  • Coulée Verte / Promenade Plantée — the original elevated park that inspired New York's High Line

Free Viewpoints

  • Sacre-Coeur steps — panoramic view of all of Paris from Montmartre hill
  • Pont Alexandre III — the most photogenic bridge with views of the Eiffel Tower, Invalides, and Grand Palais
  • Galeries Lafayette rooftop — free rooftop terrace with views of the Opera district and beyond
  • Parc de Belleville — highest park in Paris with a sweeping panorama few tourists know about

Churches and Architecture

  • Notre-Dame Cathedral — reopened in December 2024 after the fire, free entry to the nave
  • Sacre-Coeur Basilica — free entry to the main church (dome and crypt are paid)
  • Saint-Chapelle exterior — admire the Gothic masterpiece from outside (interior is paid but worth it)
  • Panthéon exterior — stunning neoclassical architecture in the Latin Quarter

Neighborhoods to Explore

  • Le Marais — medieval streets, galleries, and the Place des Vosges (Paris's oldest planned square)
  • Montmartre — artists, cafes, and the winding streets that inspired Amelie
  • Canal Saint-Martin — iron footbridges and tree-lined banks, perfect for a morning walk
  • Île de la Cité and Île Saint-Louis — the historic heart of Paris surrounded by the Seine

Frequently Asked Questions

What can you do in Paris for free?

Paris offers extensive free activities including world-class museums (Musee d'Art Moderne, Petit Palais, Musee Carnavalet), stunning parks (Luxembourg Gardens, Tuileries), iconic viewpoints (Sacre-Coeur steps, Galeries Lafayette rooftop), and beautiful neighborhoods to explore on foot.

Are any Paris museums free?

Several Paris museums have permanently free permanent collections including the Petit Palais, Musee d'Art Moderne, and Musee Carnavalet. On the first Sunday of each month, the Louvre, Musee d'Orsay, and Centre Pompidou also offer free entry. EU residents under 26 get free access to most national museums year-round.

What is the best free viewpoint in Paris?

The steps of Sacre-Coeur in Montmartre offer the most famous free panoramic view of Paris. For a less crowded alternative, the Galeries Lafayette rooftop terrace and Parc de Belleville both offer stunning views with fewer tourists.
