Best Things to Do in Rome - 2026 Guide

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Rome is the Eternal City, home to ancient ruins, Renaissance art, and world-famous cuisine. Whether you are planning your first visit or returning for more, this guide covers the 7 best things to do in Rome, Italy including prices, tips, and what to expect at each attraction.

Top 7 Things to Do in Rome

1. Colosseum (~$18)

Ancient gladiatorial arena, the largest amphitheater ever built. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

2. Vatican Museums (~$17)

Vast papal art collection culminating in the Sistine Chapel. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

3. St. Peter's Basilica (Free)

World's largest church with Michelangelo's dome (dome climb: 10 euros). Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

4. Pantheon (~$5)

Perfectly preserved 2,000-year-old Roman temple with iconic oculus. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

5. Roman Forum (~$18)

Ruins of the ancient Roman civic center, included with Colosseum ticket. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

6. Trevi Fountain (Free)

Baroque masterpiece where tradition says tossing a coin ensures your return. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

7. Borghese Gallery (~$15)

Intimate museum with Bernini sculptures and Caravaggio paintings. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Rome is April-May and September-October when weather is pleasant and crowds are manageable.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Look for a Rome city pass that bundles popular attractions at a discount
  • Book skip-the-line tickets online to save both time and money
  • Visit free attractions and parks to balance your budget
  • Eat at local spots away from the main tourist streets

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the top things to do in Rome?

Must-sees include the Colosseum, Vatican Museums and Sistine Chapel, St. Peter's Basilica, Trevi Fountain, Pantheon, and the Roman Forum. Food tours in Trastevere and day trips to Pompeii are also popular.

How do I avoid crowds at the Colosseum?

Book timed-entry tickets online in advance. Early morning (8:30 AM opening) or late afternoon slots have fewer crowds. The Roma Pass includes skip-the-line access. Consider a guided underground tour for a unique experience.

How many days do you need in Rome?

3-4 days covers the major attractions comfortably. Add a 5th day for a day trip to Pompeii, Tivoli, or the Amalfi Coast.
