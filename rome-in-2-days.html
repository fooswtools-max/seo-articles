2 Days in Rome? Here's the Itinerary Locals Recommend

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Only have 2 days in Rome? This day-by-day itinerary covers the best attractions, optimal routing, and insider tips so you do not waste a single moment in Italy's amazing capital.

At a Glance

  • Duration: 2 full days
  • Attractions covered: 6
  • Estimated attraction costs: ~$58 total (2 free attractions)
  • Best for: First-time visitors who want to see the highlights

Day 1

Morning: Colosseum (~$18)

Ancient gladiatorial arena, the largest amphitheater ever built. Allow 1.5-2 hours for this visit. Book skip-the-line tickets online to save time.

Midday: Vatican Museums (~$17)

Vast papal art collection culminating in the Sistine Chapel. Allow 1.5-2 hours for this visit. Book skip-the-line tickets online to save time.

Afternoon: St. Peter's Basilica (Free)

World's largest church with Michelangelo's dome (dome climb: 10 euros). Allow 1.5-2 hours for this visit. No tickets needed, just show up.

Evening: Explore a local neighborhood, enjoy dinner at a recommended restaurant, and soak in the Rome atmosphere.

Ruins of the ancient Roman civic center, included with Colosseum ticket. Allow 1.5-2 hours for this visit. Book skip-the-line tickets online to save time.

Practical Tips for Your 2-Day Trip

  • Book accommodation centrally: Stay near the main attractions to minimize transit time
  • Buy tickets in advance: For popular attractions, online booking saves 30-60 minutes of queuing
  • Start early: Arrive at major attractions right at opening for the smallest crowds
  • Comfortable shoes: You will walk 10-15 km per day on this itinerary
  • Get a transit pass: A 2-day public transport pass saves money and hassle

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the top things to do in Rome?

Must-sees include the Colosseum, Vatican Museums and Sistine Chapel, St. Peter's Basilica, Trevi Fountain, Pantheon, and the Roman Forum. Food tours in Trastevere and day trips to Pompeii are also popular.

How do I avoid crowds at the Colosseum?

Book timed-entry tickets online in advance. Early morning (8:30 AM opening) or late afternoon slots have fewer crowds. The Roma Pass includes skip-the-line access. Consider a guided underground tour for a unique experience.

How many days do you need in Rome?

3-4 days covers the major attractions comfortably. Add a 5th day for a day trip to Pompeii, Tivoli, or the Amalfi Coast.
