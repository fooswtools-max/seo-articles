New York Student Loan Assistance Programs 2026

Updated March 2026 · StudLoans

New York residents have access to both state-specific and federal student loan assistance programs. With average student debt of $39,000 in New York, understanding your repayment options can save you thousands of dollars.

New York Student Loan Assistance Programs

The following programs are available to New York residents:

  • NYS Get on Your Feet Loan Forgiveness
  • NYS Young Farmers Loan Forgiveness
  • District Attorney and Indigent Legal Services Attorney Loan Forgiveness

Maximum benefit: Up to $24,000 over 2 years

Federal Programs Available in New York

In addition to state programs, all New York residents can access these federal options:

  • Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) — Complete forgiveness after 10 years of qualifying public service employment
  • Income-Driven Repayment Plans — Cap payments at 5-20% of discretionary income with forgiveness after 20-25 years
  • SAVE Plan — The newest and often most generous income-driven plan
  • Teacher Loan Forgiveness — Up to $17,500 for qualifying New York teachers
  • NURSE Corps — Up to 85% of loans forgiven for nurses at shortage facilities

How to Apply for New York Programs

  1. Check eligibility — Review requirements for each New York state program
  2. Gather documents — Collect loan statements, proof of residency, and employment verification
  3. Apply for federal programs — Start at studentaid.gov for PSLF and income-driven plans
  4. Contact your state agency — Reach out to the New York higher education authority for state programs
  5. Track your progress — Keep records of all applications and qualifying payments

Tips for New York Borrowers

  • Apply for multiple programs simultaneously when eligible
  • Do not refinance federal loans with private lenders if pursuing forgiveness
  • Submit Employment Certification Forms annually for PSLF tracking
  • Consider profession-specific programs (healthcare, teaching, legal) for additional benefits
  • Stay current on annual program updates and policy changes

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Get on Your Feet program?

NYS Get on Your Feet covers up to 24 monthly student loan payments for recent graduates (within 2 years) who participate in a federal income-driven repayment plan and earn under $50,000. Maximum benefit is about $24,000 over 2 years.

Does New York offer loan forgiveness for lawyers?

Yes, New York offers loan forgiveness for District Attorneys and Indigent Legal Services attorneys. The program provides up to $6,400 per year for eligible prosecutors and public defenders working in New York.

Who qualifies for New York student loan assistance?

Eligibility varies by program. Most require New York residency, graduation from a NY college, and income limits. The Get on Your Feet program is the broadest, available to recent graduates on federal income-driven repayment plans.
