How to Invest in International Stocks in 2026 - Complete Guide

Updated March 2026 · AI How To Invest

Learning how to invest in international stocks is one of the most important financial skills you can develop in 2026. International Stocks are stocks of companies based outside the United States, and they play a critical role in building long-term wealth.

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about investing in international stocks, from getting started with your first investment to building a diversified portfolio.

What Are International Stocks?

International Stocks are stocks of companies based outside the United States. They offer investors exposure to different segments of the financial markets and can be an essential part of a well-balanced investment portfolio.

Key Facts About Investing in International Stocks

  • Risk Level: Medium to High
  • Minimum Investment: $1 (through ETFs)
  • Expected Return: 6-10% annually
  • Recommended Time Horizon: 5+ years
  • Best Platforms: Vanguard (VXUS), iShares (IXUS), Fidelity, Schwab

How to Start Investing in International Stocks

Step 1: Open a Brokerage Account

To invest in international stocks, you need a brokerage account. Top platforms for international stocks include Vanguard (VXUS), iShares (IXUS), Fidelity, Schwab. Most offer commission-free trading and no account minimums.

Step 2: Determine Your Investment Amount

You can start investing in international stocks with as little as $1 (through ETFs). The key is to invest an amount you are comfortable with and can leave invested for your target time horizon of 5+ years.

Step 3: Research and Select Your Investments

Take time to research specific international stocks before investing. Consider factors like historical performance, fees, diversification, and how they fit within your overall portfolio.

Step 4: Place Your First Order

Once you have selected your investments, place your order through your brokerage account. Consider using dollar-cost averaging to invest a fixed amount at regular intervals.

Advantages of Investing in International Stocks

  • Geographic — diversification
  • Currency — diversification
  • Access — to global growth
  • Reduced — home bias

Risks and Considerations

  • Currency risk
  • Political risk
  • Different accounting standards
  • Higher expense ratios

Tips for Success

  1. Start early — Time in the market beats timing the market.
  2. Diversify — Do not put all your money in a single international stock.
  3. Keep costs low — Choose platforms with low fees and expense ratios.
  4. Stay consistent — Regular investing through dollar-cost averaging smooths out volatility.
  5. Think long-term — International Stocks perform best when held for 5+ years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Should I invest in international stocks?

Yes, most financial advisors recommend 20-40% of your equity portfolio in international stocks for proper diversification. International stocks reduce country-specific risk and provide access to growth opportunities outside the U.S.

What is the best international stock ETF?

Top choices include VXUS (Vanguard Total International Stock), IXUS (iShares International), and SPDW (SPDR Developed World ex-US). For emerging markets specifically, consider VWO or IEMG.

Do international stocks outperform U.S. stocks?

Performance alternates in cycles. U.S. stocks outperformed from 2010-2024, but international stocks led in 2000-2009. Holding both ensures you benefit regardless of which market leads during any given period.
