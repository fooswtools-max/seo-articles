Best Free Things to Do in London in 2026

London is one of the world's best cities for free activities. Nearly all major national museums are free, the parks are spectacular, and many of the most iconic sights can be enjoyed without spending a penny.

Free World-Class Museums

  • British Museum — the Rosetta Stone, Egyptian mummies, Parthenon sculptures, and 8 million other objects
  • Natural History Museum — the blue whale skeleton, dinosaur gallery, and stunning Romanesque architecture
  • Tate Modern — world's most visited modern art museum in a converted power station on the Thames
  • Victoria and Albert Museum — the world's largest decorative arts museum with 2.3 million objects
  • National Gallery — Van Gogh, Monet, da Vinci, Rembrandt — all free, all the time
  • Science Museum — interactive exhibits, IMAX theater, and the original Stephenson's Rocket

Royal Parks

  • Hyde Park — 350 acres including Speakers' Corner, the Serpentine, and Diana Memorial Fountain
  • Regent's Park — Queen Mary's Gardens with 12,000 roses (free), plus stunning views of London
  • Greenwich Park — stand on the Prime Meridian line and enjoy views of Canary Wharf and central London
  • Richmond Park — 2,500 acres with wild deer herds, the largest urban park in Europe

Free Iconic Experiences

  • Changing of the Guard — Buckingham Palace, 11 AM (Mon/Wed/Fri/Sun, daily in summer)
  • Borough Market — free to browse one of London's oldest and most famous food markets
  • Sky Garden — free observation deck on the 35th floor (must book ahead at skygarden.london)
  • Southbank Walk — from Westminster Bridge to Tower Bridge along the Thames (3 miles of landmarks)

Free Neighborhoods to Explore

  • Covent Garden — free street performers, historic market halls, and people-watching
  • Notting Hill — pastel-colored houses, Portobello Road Market (Saturdays), and independent bookshops
  • Shoreditch — street art, vintage shops, and the creative east London scene
  • Camden — eclectic markets, canal walks, and live music spilling out of pubs

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best free museums in London?

London's top free museums include the British Museum, Natural History Museum, Tate Modern, National Gallery, Victoria and Albert Museum, and Science Museum. All permanent collections are completely free, making London arguably the best city in the world for free museum visits.

Is the Changing of the Guard free?

Yes, watching the Changing of the Guard at Buckingham Palace is completely free. It takes place at 11 AM on Monday, Wednesday, Friday, and Sunday (daily during summer months). Arrive by 10:15 AM for a good viewing spot near the palace gates.

What is the best free viewpoint in London?

Sky Garden at 20 Fenchurch Street offers the best free panoramic views of London from the 35th floor. Entry is free but you must book a timed slot in advance at skygarden.london. The terrace at One New Change shopping center also offers free views of St Paul's Cathedral.
