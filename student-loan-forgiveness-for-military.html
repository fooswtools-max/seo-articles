Student Loan Forgiveness for Militarys - 2026 Guide

Updated March 2026 · StudLoans

As a military, you may qualify for significant student loan forgiveness through federal and state programs designed specifically for your profession. This guide covers every forgiveness option available to military in 2026.

Student Loan Forgiveness Programs for Military

Military have access to several loan forgiveness and repayment assistance programs:

  • Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)
  • Income-Driven Repayment Forgiveness
  • State Military loan assistance programs

How Much Can Military Get Forgiven?

Depending on the program and your loan balance, military can receive $20,000 - $100,000+ in student loan forgiveness. The timeline typically ranges from 5-25 years.

Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for student loan forgiveness as a military, you generally need to meet these requirements:

  • Work at a qualifying employer
  • Make 120 qualifying payments
  • Be on an income-driven repayment plan

Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)

PSLF is one of the most valuable programs available to military. After making 120 qualifying payments while working full-time for a qualifying employer, your remaining federal loan balance is completely forgiven, tax-free.

How to Qualify for PSLF

  1. Consolidate any FFEL or Perkins loans into a Direct Consolidation Loan
  2. Enroll in an income-driven repayment plan (SAVE, PAYE, IBR, or ICR)
  3. Work full-time at a qualifying public service employer
  4. Submit the PSLF Employment Certification Form annually
  5. Apply for forgiveness after 120 qualifying payments

Income-Driven Repayment Forgiveness

Even without PSLF, military on income-driven repayment plans receive forgiveness after 20-25 years of qualifying payments. The SAVE plan offers the most generous terms for most borrowers.

Action Steps for Military

  1. Inventory your loans — Log into studentaid.gov to see all federal loans
  2. Check your employer — Verify your employer qualifies using the PSLF Help Tool
  3. Choose the right plan — Use the Loan Simulator to find your optimal repayment plan
  4. Submit your ECF — File your Employment Certification Form to start tracking payments
  5. Explore state programs — Check your state for additional profession-specific programs
