Best Time to Visit Paris in 2026: Month-by-Month Guide

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Planning a trip to Paris, France? The best time to visit Paris is April-June and September-October when you get pleasant weather, manageable crowds, and the best combination of activities and prices.

Month-by-Month Guide to Paris

Peak Season (Summer)

Summer brings the longest days and warmest weather, but also the biggest crowds and highest prices. If visiting in peak season, book accommodations 2-3 months in advance and buy attraction tickets online to skip long lines.

Shoulder Season (Spring & Fall)

This is our recommended time to visit. You get comfortable temperatures, fewer tourists, lower hotel prices, and full access to all attractions. April-June and September-October offers the ideal balance of weather and value.

Off-Season (Winter)

Winter means the fewest tourists and lowest prices, but shorter days and potentially cold or rainy weather. Some attractions may have reduced hours. However, holiday markets and festive atmosphere can make winter visits magical.

Top Attractions in Paris

  • Eiffel Tower — Iconic iron tower with panoramic city views from three observation levels
  • Louvre Museum — World's largest art museum, home to the Mona Lisa and 35,000+ works
  • Musee d'Orsay — Impressionist masterpieces in a stunning converted railway station
  • Palace of Versailles — Opulent royal palace with Hall of Mirrors and formal gardens
  • Sacre-Coeur — White-domed basilica atop Montmartre with sweeping Paris views

Budget Tip

The average daily budget for Paris is $100-200 per person, including accommodation, food, transport, and attractions. Visit during shoulder season to save 20-30% on hotels.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the must-see attractions in Paris?

The top attractions include the Eiffel Tower, Louvre Museum, Notre-Dame Cathedral, Sacre-Coeur, Musee d'Orsay, and the Palace of Versailles as a day trip. Paris also offers incredible food tours, Seine river cruises, and neighborhood walks.

How many days do you need in Paris?

3-5 days is ideal for first-time visitors. This allows time for major landmarks, a museum day, a food tour, and a day trip to Versailles or Giverny.

Is Paris expensive for tourists?

Paris can be moderate to expensive. Many top museums offer free entry for under-26 EU residents. Free attractions include the Sacre-Coeur, Notre-Dame exterior, parks, and walking neighborhoods like Le Marais and Montmartre.
