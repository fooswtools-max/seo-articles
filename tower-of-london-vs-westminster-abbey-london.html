Tower of London or Westminster Abbey First? Here's What Most London Tourists Get Wrong

Updated May 2026 · AttractionScout

Trying to decide between Tower of London and Westminster Abbey in London? Both are top attractions, but they offer very different experiences. This comparison breaks down cost, time needed, best for whom, and our verdict to help you choose (or visit both).

Tower of London: What to Expect

Historic castle housing the Crown Jewels and 900 years of history. Tower of London is one of the most popular attractions in London and attracts millions of visitors each year. Tickets cost approximately $33 per adult.

Visitors typically spend 1.5 to 3 hours here. We recommend arriving early in the morning or late in the afternoon to avoid the largest crowds. Book skip-the-line tickets online in advance to save time.

Westminster Abbey: What to Expect

Gothic abbey where monarchs are crowned and notable Britons are buried. Westminster Abbey offers a different but equally rewarding experience in London. Tickets cost approximately $27 per adult.

Plan for 1.5 to 3 hours to fully appreciate what Westminster Abbey has to offer. Like most popular London attractions, booking in advance is strongly recommended.

Price Comparison

Westminster Abbey is the more affordable option, saving you approximately $6 per person.

Both attractions offer excellent value for the experience. If budget is a concern, consider purchasing a city pass or combo ticket that includes both at a discount.

Our Verdict: Which Should You Choose?

If you only have time for one, choose Tower of London if you prefer landmark experiences, or Westminster Abbey if you lean toward landmark attractions. But honestly? If your schedule allows, visit both — they complement each other perfectly and together give you a well-rounded London experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the top free things to do in London?

London has many free world-class museums including the British Museum, National Gallery, Tate Modern, Natural History Museum, and Victoria and Albert Museum. Walking along the South Bank, visiting Hyde Park, and watching the Changing of the Guard are also free.

How many days do you need in London?

4-5 days is ideal. This covers major landmarks, museums, a show in the West End, and day trips to Stonehenge or Windsor Castle.

Is London expensive for tourists?

London can be expensive but many top attractions are free. Budget travelers can eat well at markets like Borough Market, use the Oyster card for transport, and enjoy free museums. A mid-range daily budget is $120-250.
