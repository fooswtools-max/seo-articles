How I Did Amsterdam for Under $50/Day — 2026 Budget Secrets

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Think Amsterdam is too expensive? Think again. With the right planning, you can experience the best of Amsterdam, Netherlands without breaking the bank. This guide covers free attractions, cheap eats, money-saving transport tips, and budget accommodation.

Free Things to Do in Amsterdam

  • Vondelpark (Free) — Amsterdam's beloved urban park perfect for cycling and picnicking

Many of Amsterdam's best experiences cost nothing — walking through historic neighborhoods, enjoying parks, people-watching at famous squares, and exploring street markets.

Cheap Attractions Under $15

  • Look for discounted combo tickets and city passes

Budget Food Tips

  • Street food and markets: The cheapest and often most authentic meals
  • Lunch specials: Many restaurants offer set lunch menus at 30-50% less than dinner
  • Supermarkets: Pick up breakfast supplies and snacks at local grocery stores
  • Water bottle: Carry a reusable bottle to avoid buying drinks at tourist prices
  • Eat where locals eat: Venture two blocks from any major attraction for better prices

Money-Saving Transport Tips

  • Day passes: Buy a public transport day pass instead of individual tickets
  • Walk: Most city centers are walkable — you will see more and save money
  • Avoid taxis from the airport: Use public transport or pre-booked shuttles
  • Bike rental: Many cities have affordable bike-sharing programs

Budget Accommodation Tips

  • Stay slightly outside the center: Hotels 2-3 metro stops from the center are 30-50% cheaper
  • Book 4-6 weeks ahead: Best balance of availability and price
  • Consider hostels with private rooms: Often $30-50/night with hotel-like privacy
  • Apartments with kitchens: Save on meals by cooking breakfast at home

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the must-see attractions in Amsterdam?

Top attractions include the Anne Frank House, Rijksmuseum, Van Gogh Museum, a canal cruise, Vondelpark, and exploring the Jordaan neighborhood. The Heineken Experience and Albert Cuyp Market are also popular.

How do I get tickets to the Anne Frank House?

Tickets are released online about 6 weeks in advance and sell out quickly. Check the official website on the release date. A small number of tickets are released each day for same-day visits.

Is Amsterdam walkable?

Amsterdam is very walkable and even more bicycle-friendly. Most major attractions are within 30 minutes walk of each other. Renting a bike is the most authentic way to explore the city.
