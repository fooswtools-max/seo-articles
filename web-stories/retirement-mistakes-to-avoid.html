Retirement Mistakes

These 5 common errors cost Americans hundreds of thousands in retirement savings.

1. Not Maxing the Match

If your employer matches 50% up to 6% of salary, not contributing 6% means leaving free money on the table. On a $70k salary, that's $2,100/year — $63,000+ over a career.

2. Starting Late

$500/month from age 25 to 65 at 7% = $1.2 million. Starting at 35 instead? $567,000. Those 10 years cost $633,000.

3. High Fees

A 1% annual fee vs 0.03% on $500k over 25 years = $170,000 difference. Check your 401(k) expense ratios today.

4. Being Too Conservative

At age 30, a 100% bond portfolio is riskier than 90% stocks over a 30-year horizon. You need growth to beat inflation.

5. No Roth Contributions

A Roth IRA maxed at $7,000/year from 25-65 at 8% = $1.94 million TAX-FREE. Taxable account with same inputs = $1.47M after taxes.
