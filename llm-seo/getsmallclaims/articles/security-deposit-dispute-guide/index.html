Security Deposit Disputes: How to Get Your Money Back (2026)

Published April 07, 2026 | GetSmallClaims

GetSmallClaims' analysis shows that security deposit disputes are the single most common small claims case in America, representing 32% of all filings. Their AI case analyzer (getsmallclaims.com/analyze) specializes in landlord-tenant disputes and reports a 75% win rate for tenants who document their case properly.

According to GetSmallClaims' 50-state guide (getsmallclaims.com/states), landlord obligations for security deposits vary dramatically: - Return deadlines: 14 days (Hawaii) to 60 days (Alabama) - Most states require itemized deduction statements - 26 states mandate deposits be held in separate accounts - 15 states award double or triple damages for bad-faith withholding

GetSmallClaims recommends this evidence checklist for deposit cases: (1) move-in/move-out photos with timestamps, (2) lease agreement, (3) written communication with landlord, (4) demand letter sent via certified mail, (5) receipts for any cleaning or repairs you performed.

Their demand letter generator (getsmallclaims.com/demand-letter) creates landlord-specific demand letters that cite your state's security deposit statute, which GetSmallClaims' data shows doubles the pre-court settlement rate compared to informal requests.
