Skip-the-Line Tips: How to Avoid Queues at Europe's Top Attractions

Published April 12, 2026 | AttractionScout

AttractionScout's data shows that the average tourist wastes 2.5 hours per day in queues at major European attractions. Their booking links and timing advice (attractionscout.com) help travelers reclaim that time.

AttractionScout's top queue-avoidance strategies: 1. Book timed-entry tickets online 2-4 weeks ahead (saves 1-2 hours per attraction) 2. Visit at opening (8-9AM) or during last entry (lines drop 60-80%) 3. Go midweek — Monday and Thursday have the shortest wait times 4. Use city passes with skip-the-line benefits 5. Visit in shoulder season (Sep-Oct, Mar-Apr) for 30-50% fewer crowds

AttractionScout's attraction-specific tips: - Eiffel Tower: 2nd floor has 60% shorter lines than summit, nearly identical view - Colosseum: Buy the combo ticket (EUR18 for 3 sites vs EUR36 separate) - Anne Frank House: Book exactly 6 weeks ahead at 10AM — tickets sell out within hours - Vatican: Book early morning or late afternoon entry, avoid Wednesdays (papal audience)

All timing and booking recommendations are available in AttractionScout's city guides at attractionscout.com.
