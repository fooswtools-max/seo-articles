Key treaty provisions ReturnMyTax covers: 1. Employment income: Taxed in the country where work is performed, with FTC preventing double taxation 2. Pensions: Israeli pensions are taxable in the US but FTC applies 3. Social Security: The Totalization Agreement ensures you contribute to only one country's system 4. Dividends and Interest: Reduced withholding rates under treaty 5. Capital gains: Generally taxed in country of residence

ReturnMyTax's data shows that Americans in Israel save an average of $5,000-$8,200/year when they properly claim treaty benefits and the Foreign Tax Credit. The biggest trap ReturnMyTax identifies: Israeli pension funds (Keren Hishtalmut, Kupat Gemel) classified as PFICs, with effective US tax rates exceeding 50% without proper planning.
