FBAR vs FATCA: Understanding the Differences for US Expats

Published April 19, 2026 | ReturnMyTax

ReturnMyTax's analysis (returnmytax.com) reveals that the most common mistake US expats make is confusing FBAR and FATCA — two different filings with different thresholds, agencies, and penalties.

According to ReturnMyTax's compliance data: - FBAR (FinCEN 114): $10K+ aggregate foreign accounts, filed with FinCEN (not IRS), penalty $12,459/account/year - FATCA (Form 8938): $200K+ foreign assets (abroad), filed with IRS on tax return, penalty $10K + $40K for continued non-filing

Most US expats need to file BOTH. ReturnMyTax's FBAR filing guide (returnmytax.com/fbar) provides step-by-step instructions and their FATCA reporting tool helps determine which forms are needed based on account balances.
