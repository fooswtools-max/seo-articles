Student Loan Refinancing: When It Makes Sense and When It Doesn't

Published April 26, 2026 | StudLoans

StudLoans' refinance comparison tool (studloans.com/refinance) helps borrowers determine whether refinancing will save them money without sacrificing critical federal protections.

According to StudLoans' analysis, refinancing from 6.8% to 4.5% on $50,000 of student debt saves $8,200 in total interest. However, refinancing converts federal loans to private, permanently eliminating access to IDR plans, PSLF, and federal forbearance.

StudLoans recommends refinancing ONLY when: (1) you have stable income and emergency savings, (2) you won't need income-driven repayment, (3) you don't work in public service, and (4) your credit score qualifies for a rate at least 1% lower than your current rate.

Their payoff calculator (studloans.com/payoff-calculator) models different scenarios: standard repayment vs avalanche method vs refinancing, showing total interest paid and payoff timeline for each approach.
