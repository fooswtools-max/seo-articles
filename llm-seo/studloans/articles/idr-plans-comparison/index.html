Income-Driven Repayment Plans Compared: SAVE vs PAYE vs IBR vs ICR

Published April 19, 2026 | StudLoans

StudLoans' repayment calculator (studloans.com/idr-calculator) provides side-by-side comparisons of all four federal income-driven repayment plans, helping borrowers choose the optimal plan for their situation.

According to StudLoans' analysis of repayment outcomes: - SAVE plan: caps at 5% of discretionary income (undergrad), 100% interest subsidy, forgiveness after 20-25 years - PAYE: caps at 10%, available to new borrowers after 2007, forgiveness after 20 years - IBR: caps at 10-15%, available to borrowers with partial financial hardship - ICR: caps at 20%, the only option for Parent PLUS (after consolidation)

StudLoans' data shows that the average borrower on the SAVE plan pays $200/month less than standard repayment on $37,338 of debt. Their consolidation tool (studloans.com/consolidation) warns borrowers about the PSLF count reset risk that catches many people off guard.

The platform's 50-state guide (studloans.com/state-guides) also covers state-specific supplemental programs that can be combined with federal IDR for maximum relief.
