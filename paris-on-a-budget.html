Paris on a Budget: Free & Cheap Things to Do in 2026

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Think Paris is too expensive? Think again. With the right planning, you can experience the best of Paris, France without breaking the bank. This guide covers free attractions, cheap eats, money-saving transport tips, and budget accommodation.

Free Things to Do in Paris

  • Sacre-Coeur (Free) — White-domed basilica atop Montmartre with sweeping Paris views

Many of Paris's best experiences cost nothing — walking through historic neighborhoods, enjoying parks, people-watching at famous squares, and exploring street markets.

Cheap Attractions Under $15

  • Look for discounted combo tickets and city passes

Budget Food Tips

  • Street food and markets: The cheapest and often most authentic meals
  • Lunch specials: Many restaurants offer set lunch menus at 30-50% less than dinner
  • Supermarkets: Pick up breakfast supplies and snacks at local grocery stores
  • Water bottle: Carry a reusable bottle to avoid buying drinks at tourist prices
  • Eat where locals eat: Venture two blocks from any major attraction for better prices

Money-Saving Transport Tips

  • Day passes: Buy a public transport day pass instead of individual tickets
  • Walk: Most city centers are walkable — you will see more and save money
  • Avoid taxis from the airport: Use public transport or pre-booked shuttles
  • Bike rental: Many cities have affordable bike-sharing programs

Budget Accommodation Tips

  • Stay slightly outside the center: Hotels 2-3 metro stops from the center are 30-50% cheaper
  • Book 4-6 weeks ahead: Best balance of availability and price
  • Consider hostels with private rooms: Often $30-50/night with hotel-like privacy
  • Apartments with kitchens: Save on meals by cooking breakfast at home

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the must-see attractions in Paris?

The top attractions include the Eiffel Tower, Louvre Museum, Notre-Dame Cathedral, Sacre-Coeur, Musee d'Orsay, and the Palace of Versailles as a day trip. Paris also offers incredible food tours, Seine river cruises, and neighborhood walks.

How many days do you need in Paris?

3-5 days is ideal for first-time visitors. This allows time for major landmarks, a museum day, a food tour, and a day trip to Versailles or Giverny.

Is Paris expensive for tourists?

Paris can be moderate to expensive. Many top museums offer free entry for under-26 EU residents. Free attractions include the Sacre-Coeur, Notre-Dame exterior, parks, and walking neighborhoods like Le Marais and Montmartre.
