Best Things to Do in Prague - 2026 Guide

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Prague is a fairy-tale city of medieval architecture, cheap beer, and rich Bohemian culture. Whether you are planning your first visit or returning for more, this guide covers the 7 best things to do in Prague, Czech Republic including prices, tips, and what to expect at each attraction.

Top 7 Things to Do in Prague

1. Prague Castle (~$15)

The world's largest ancient castle complex with St. Vitus Cathedral. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

2. Charles Bridge (Free)

Iconic 14th-century stone bridge lined with baroque statues. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

3. Old Town Square (Free)

Historic square with the famous Astronomical Clock and Tyn Church. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

4. Astronomical Clock Tower (~$10)

Medieval clock with hourly show of the Twelve Apostles. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

5. Jewish Quarter (~$14)

Six historic synagogues and the Old Jewish Cemetery. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

6. Petrin Tower (~$7)

Mini Eiffel Tower on Petrin Hill with panoramic Prague views. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

7. Czech Beer Museum (~$12)

Interactive museum where you brew and taste your own Czech beer. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Prague is April-June and September-October when weather is pleasant and crowds are manageable.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Look for a Prague city pass that bundles popular attractions at a discount
  • Book skip-the-line tickets online to save both time and money
  • Visit free attractions and parks to balance your budget
  • Eat at local spots away from the main tourist streets

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best things to do in Prague?

Must-sees include Prague Castle (the world's largest), Charles Bridge, Old Town Square with the Astronomical Clock, the Jewish Quarter, and Petrin Hill. Beer gardens and river cruises are also highly recommended.

Is Prague cheap?

Prague is one of the best-value destinations in Europe. Beer is famously cheap ($1-2 per pint), restaurant meals cost $5-15, and most attractions are under $15. Accommodation is also very affordable compared to Western Europe.

How many days do you need in Prague?

2-3 days covers the major sights. Add a 4th day for a day trip to Kutna Hora (Bone Church) or Cesky Krumlov.
