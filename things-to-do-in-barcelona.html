Best Things to Do in Barcelona - 2026 Guide

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Barcelona is a vibrant coastal city famous for Gaudi architecture, beaches, and Mediterranean cuisine. Whether you are planning your first visit or returning for more, this guide covers the 7 best things to do in Barcelona, Spain including prices, tips, and what to expect at each attraction.

Top 7 Things to Do in Barcelona

1. La Sagrada Familia (~$26)

Gaudi's unfinished masterpiece basilica, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

2. Park Guell (~$10)

Whimsical Gaudi-designed park with mosaic terraces and city views. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

3. Casa Batllo (~$35)

Gaudi's fantastical apartment building with dragon-scale roof. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

4. La Boqueria Market (Free)

Vibrant food market on La Rambla with fresh produce and tapas. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

5. Camp Nou (~$28)

FC Barcelona's legendary stadium with museum and pitch-side tours. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

6. Gothic Quarter (Free)

Medieval neighborhood with narrow streets, plazas, and hidden bars. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

7. Montjuic Cable Car (~$13)

Cable car ride with stunning harbor and city panorama. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Barcelona is May-June and September-October when weather is pleasant and crowds are manageable.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Look for a Barcelona city pass that bundles popular attractions at a discount
  • Book skip-the-line tickets online to save both time and money
  • Visit free attractions and parks to balance your budget
  • Eat at local spots away from the main tourist streets

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best attractions in Barcelona?

Top attractions include La Sagrada Familia, Park Guell, Casa Batllo, La Boqueria Market, the Gothic Quarter, and Barceloneta Beach. Day trips to Montserrat and Costa Brava are also highly recommended.

How do I get tickets to La Sagrada Familia?

Book tickets online at least 2-3 weeks in advance, especially for peak season. Timed entry tickets cost around 26 euros. Morning visits have the best interior light. Skip-the-line tickets are available through the official website.

Is Barcelona good for families?

Excellent for families. The beach, Park Guell, the Aquarium, Tibidabo amusement park, and the Magic Fountain light show are all kid-friendly. The city is walkable and public transport is easy.
