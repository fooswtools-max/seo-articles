How I Did Bangkok for Under $50/Day — 2026 Budget Secrets

Updated May 2026 · AttractionScout

Think Bangkok is too expensive? Think again. With the right planning, you can experience the best of Bangkok, Thailand without breaking the bank. This guide covers free attractions, cheap eats, money-saving transport tips, and budget accommodation.

Free Things to Do in Bangkok

  • Chatuchak Weekend Market (Free) — One of the world's largest markets with 15,000+ stalls

Many of Bangkok's best experiences cost nothing — walking through historic neighborhoods, enjoying parks, people-watching at famous squares, and exploring street markets.

Cheap Attractions Under $15

  • Grand Palace ($15) — Former royal residence with the revered Emerald Buddha temple
  • Wat Pho ($6) — Home to a giant 46-meter reclining gold Buddha and traditional massage school
  • Wat Arun ($3) — Temple of Dawn with a towering spire decorated in Chinese porcelain
  • Floating Market ($10) — Traditional market where vendors sell food and goods from boats
  • Jim Thompson House ($6) — Preserved Thai silk merchant's home with traditional architecture and art
  • Rooftop Bar ($15) — Sky-high cocktails at famous bars like Sirocco, Octave, or Vertigo

Budget Food Tips

  • Street food and markets: The cheapest and often most authentic meals
  • Lunch specials: Many restaurants offer set lunch menus at 30-50% less than dinner
  • Supermarkets: Pick up breakfast supplies and snacks at local grocery stores
  • Water bottle: Carry a reusable bottle to avoid buying drinks at tourist prices
  • Eat where locals eat: Venture two blocks from any major attraction for better prices

Money-Saving Transport Tips

  • Day passes: Buy a public transport day pass instead of individual tickets
  • Walk: Most city centers are walkable — you will see more and save money
  • Avoid taxis from the airport: Use public transport or pre-booked shuttles
  • Bike rental: Many cities have affordable bike-sharing programs

Budget Accommodation Tips

  • Stay slightly outside the center: Hotels 2-3 metro stops from the center are 30-50% cheaper
  • Book 4-6 weeks ahead: Best balance of availability and price
  • Consider hostels with private rooms: Often $30-50/night with hotel-like privacy
  • Apartments with kitchens: Save on meals by cooking breakfast at home

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best things to do in Bangkok?

Top attractions include the Grand Palace, Wat Pho (Reclining Buddha), Wat Arun, Chatuchak Weekend Market, floating markets, street food tours in Chinatown, and river cruises. Night markets and rooftop bars are also popular.

Is Bangkok cheap for tourists?

Bangkok is one of the most affordable major cities. Street food meals cost $1-3, taxi rides are very cheap, and many temples cost just a few dollars. A comfortable daily budget is $30-80 including accommodation.

Is Bangkok safe for tourists?

Bangkok is generally safe for tourists. Standard precautions apply: watch for pickpockets in crowded areas, use official taxis or Grab, and be aware of common tourist scams. The city has excellent tourist police and healthcare facilities.
