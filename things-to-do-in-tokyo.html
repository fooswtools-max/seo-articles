Best Things to Do in Tokyo - 2026 Guide

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Tokyo is a futuristic megacity blending ancient temples, cutting-edge technology, and incredible food. Whether you are planning your first visit or returning for more, this guide covers the 8 best things to do in Tokyo, Japan including prices, tips, and what to expect at each attraction.

Top 8 Things to Do in Tokyo

1. Tokyo Skytree (~$21)

Japan's tallest structure with observation decks at 350m and 450m. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

2. Senso-ji Temple (Free)

Tokyo's oldest and most famous Buddhist temple in Asakusa. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

3. Meiji Shrine (Free)

Serene Shinto shrine in a forested park near Harajuku. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

4. TeamLab Borderless (~$27)

Immersive digital art museum with rooms of interactive projections. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

5. Tokyo Tower (~$12)

Eiffel-inspired tower with observation deck and Tokyo cityscape views. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

6. Tsukiji Outer Market (Free)

Legendary seafood market with the freshest sushi and street food. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

7. Shibuya Crossing (Free)

World's busiest pedestrian crossing, an icon of modern Tokyo. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

8. Robot Restaurant (~$60)

Wild neon-lit show with dancing robots and performers in Shinjuku. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Tokyo is March-May (cherry blossom) and October-November when weather is pleasant and crowds are manageable.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Look for a Tokyo city pass that bundles popular attractions at a discount
  • Book skip-the-line tickets online to save both time and money
  • Visit free attractions and parks to balance your budget
  • Eat at local spots away from the main tourist streets

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best attractions in Tokyo?

Top attractions include Senso-ji Temple in Asakusa, Shibuya Crossing, Meiji Shrine, Tsukiji Outer Market, Akihabara, Tokyo Skytree, and TeamLab Borderless. Day trips to Mount Fuji, Kamakura, and Nikko are highly recommended.

Is Tokyo expensive for tourists?

Tokyo is moderate. Street food and ramen are very affordable ($5-10 per meal). Many temples and shrines are free. Hotels range widely. The Japan Rail Pass saves money on day trips.

How many days do you need in Tokyo?

4-5 days is ideal. This covers major neighborhoods, temples, food markets, and allows for 1-2 day trips. Tokyo is large, so focus on 2-3 areas per day.
