Small Claims Court in Pennsylvania: What You Need to Know

Small claims court in Pennsylvania handles disputes up to $12,000. It is designed for regular people to resolve disputes quickly and affordably — no lawyer required. Common cases include security deposit disputes, unpaid debts, property damage, contractor issues, and consumer complaints.

Pennsylvania Note: Called 'minor court' in PA. Appeals go to Court of Common Pleas for a new trial.

Step-by-Step Filing Process

Step 1: Send a Demand Letter

Formally Request Payment

Before filing, Pennsylvania courts expect you to make a written demand. Send a letter stating what is owed, why, and a deadline (usually 14-30 days). Keep a copy — judges look favorably on plaintiffs who tried to resolve things first.

Step 2: Gather Your Evidence

Build Your Case

Collect all relevant documentation: contracts, receipts, photos, text messages, emails, witness contact info, and any written communication. Organize everything chronologically. The stronger your paper trail, the stronger your case.

  • Contracts or lease agreements
  • Photos/videos of damage or condition
  • Receipts and payment records
  • Text messages and emails
  • Witness statements
Step 3: File Your Claim

Submit to Magisterial District Court

File your small claims case at the Magisterial District Court in Pennsylvania. The filing fee ranges from $45-$100. You will need the defendant's full legal name and address. Many Pennsylvania counties now offer online filing.

Step 4: Serve the Defendant

Official Notification

The defendant must be officially served with notice of the lawsuit. In Pennsylvania, this can typically be done by certified mail, a process server, or the sheriff's office. You cannot serve the papers yourself.

Step 5: Present Your Case

Your Day in Court

Arrive early, dress professionally, and bring all your evidence organized. Be concise and factual. Address the judge directly. Present your evidence in order and explain clearly what happened and what you are owed.

Common Small Claims Cases in Pennsylvania

  • Security deposit disputes — Landlord has 30 days for security deposits to return your deposit in Pennsylvania
  • Unpaid debts — Someone borrowed money and will not pay it back
  • Contractor disputes — Work not completed or done poorly
  • Property damage — Neighbor, tenant, or accident damage
  • Consumer complaints — Defective products, services not rendered
  • Car accident damage — Minor collision claims under the limit

Tips for Winning in Pennsylvania Small Claims Court

  1. Document everything — Photos, screenshots, receipts. If it is not documented, it did not happen.
  2. Be organized — Create a timeline of events. Judges appreciate clarity.
  3. Stay calm and factual — Emotional arguments hurt your case. Stick to facts and evidence.
  4. Bring copies — One set for you, one for the judge, one for the defendant.
  5. Know your numbers — Calculate exactly what you are owed and be ready to justify each dollar.
