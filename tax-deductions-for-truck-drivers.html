Tax Deductions for Truck Driverss - Complete 2026 Guide

Updated March 2026 · ReturnMyTax

As a truck driver, you likely have numerous tax deductions available that could save you thousands of dollars each year. This guide covers every major deduction truck drivers can claim on their 2026 tax returns.

Average potential savings: $2,000 - $10,000 per year

Top Tax Deductions for Truck Drivers

  • Home office deduction
  • Business expenses
  • Professional development
  • Equipment and supplies
  • Mileage and travel

How Tax Deductions Work for Truck Drivers

Tax deductions reduce your taxable income, which lowers the amount of tax you owe. For truck drivers, deductions are typically claimed on Schedule C (self-employed) or as adjustments to income on Form 1040.

Special rules: Standard self-employment tax rules apply

Detailed Deduction Breakdown

Business Expenses

Any expense that is ordinary and necessary for your work as a truck driver is deductible. This includes supplies, equipment, software subscriptions, and professional services directly related to generating income.

Home Office Deduction

If you use part of your home exclusively for business, you can deduct either $5 per square foot (up to $1,500 with the simplified method) or your actual expenses proportional to your office space.

Vehicle and Mileage

For 2026, the standard mileage rate is used for business driving. Track all business miles using an app or mileage log. You can deduct either the standard mileage rate or actual vehicle expenses.

Professional Development

Courses, certifications, conferences, books, and subscriptions that maintain or improve your skills as a truck driver are fully deductible.

Health Insurance

Self-employed truck drivers can deduct 100% of health, dental, and long-term care insurance premiums for themselves and their families, reducing adjusted gross income.

Tax Filing Tips for Truck Drivers

  1. Track everything — Use accounting software to categorize expenses throughout the year
  2. Keep receipts — Save receipts for all business purchases over $75
  3. Separate accounts — Use a dedicated business bank account and credit card
  4. Estimate quarterly — Pay estimated taxes quarterly to avoid penalties
  5. Consider a CPA — Professional tax preparation often pays for itself in found deductions
