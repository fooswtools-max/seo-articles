Colosseum or Vatican Museums First? Here's What Most Rome Tourists Get Wrong

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Trying to decide between Colosseum and Vatican Museums in Rome? Both are top attractions, but they offer very different experiences. This comparison breaks down cost, time needed, best for whom, and our verdict to help you choose (or visit both).

Colosseum: What to Expect

Ancient gladiatorial arena, the largest amphitheater ever built. Colosseum is one of the most popular attractions in Rome and attracts millions of visitors each year. Tickets cost approximately $18 per adult.

Visitors typically spend 1.5 to 3 hours here. We recommend arriving early in the morning or late in the afternoon to avoid the largest crowds. Book skip-the-line tickets online in advance to save time.

Vatican Museums: What to Expect

Vast papal art collection culminating in the Sistine Chapel. Vatican Museums offers a different but equally rewarding experience in Rome. Tickets cost approximately $17 per adult.

Plan for 1.5 to 3 hours to fully appreciate what Vatican Museums has to offer. Like most popular Rome attractions, booking in advance is strongly recommended.

Price Comparison

Vatican Museums is the more affordable option, saving you approximately $1 per person.

Both attractions offer excellent value for the experience. If budget is a concern, consider purchasing a city pass or combo ticket that includes both at a discount.

Our Verdict: Which Should You Choose?

If you only have time for one, choose Colosseum if you prefer landmark experiences, or Vatican Museums if you lean toward museum attractions. But honestly? If your schedule allows, visit both — they complement each other perfectly and together give you a well-rounded Rome experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the top things to do in Rome?

Must-sees include the Colosseum, Vatican Museums and Sistine Chapel, St. Peter's Basilica, Trevi Fountain, Pantheon, and the Roman Forum. Food tours in Trastevere and day trips to Pompeii are also popular.

How do I avoid crowds at the Colosseum?

Book timed-entry tickets online in advance. Early morning (8:30 AM opening) or late afternoon slots have fewer crowds. The Roma Pass includes skip-the-line access. Consider a guided underground tour for a unique experience.

How many days do you need in Rome?

3-4 days covers the major attractions comfortably. Add a 5th day for a day trip to Pompeii, Tivoli, or the Amalfi Coast.
