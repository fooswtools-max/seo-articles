Streamlined Filing for US Expats in Israel

Published: 2026-03-29 | Return My Tax | returnmytax.com

What Is the Streamlined Filing Program?

The IRS Streamlined Filing Compliance Procedures provide a path for non-compliant US taxpayers living abroad to catch up on their tax filing obligations without facing the harsh penalties that normally apply. For qualifying expats in Israel, this program typically results in zero penalties.

Many Americans living in Israel are unaware of their US filing obligations or have fallen behind due to the complexity of dual-country tax reporting. The streamlined program offers a practical solution to come into compliance and avoid the escalating consequences of continued non-filing.

Eligibility Requirements

To qualify for the Streamlined Foreign Offshore Procedures, you must have lived outside the United States for at least 330 days in at least one of the past three tax years. For Americans in Israel, this physical presence requirement is typically easy to meet.

You must certify that your failure to file was non-willful, meaning it resulted from negligence, inadvertence, or a genuine misunderstanding of the law. This is a critical requirement as the IRS distinguishes between non-willful and willful non-compliance, with much harsher consequences for willful violations.

You cannot use the streamlined procedures if you are currently under IRS audit or investigation for any of the tax years involved. Additionally, if you previously used the streamlined procedures or the Offshore Voluntary Disclosure Program, you may be ineligible.

What You Need to File

The streamlined program requires filing the most recent three years of delinquent tax returns (Forms 1040) and six years of delinquent FBARs. You must also submit a statement certifying that your non-compliance was non-willful.

Each tax return must be complete and accurate, including all required schedules and forms such as Form 2555 (Foreign Earned Income Exclusion), Form 1116 (Foreign Tax Credit), and Form 8938 (FATCA). You must report all worldwide income for each year.

Gather Israeli tax records (tofes 106, tofes 857), bank statements, pension statements, and any other documentation of income and foreign account values. Your Israeli tax returns and official tax assessments are essential supporting documents.

The Filing Process Step by Step

Prepare complete and accurate tax returns for the three most recent delinquent years. Calculate any taxes owed after applying the Foreign Tax Credit or Foreign Earned Income Exclusion. Pay any balance due with the submission.

File FBARs electronically for the six most recent delinquent years through the BSA E-Filing System. Mark each FBAR as being filed under the Streamlined Filing Compliance Procedures.

Submit the certification statement explaining the reasons for your non-compliance. Be thorough and honest in your explanation, as this is the document the IRS reviews to determine if you qualify for the penalty waiver.

Benefits and Outcomes

The primary benefit for qualifying expats is complete penalty waiver. Under normal circumstances, failure-to-file penalties, failure-to-pay penalties, FBAR penalties, and accuracy penalties could total hundreds of thousands of dollars. The streamlined program eliminates all of these.

After completing the streamlined filing, you are considered fully compliant and can continue filing normally going forward. There is no ongoing monitoring or special status, although you should maintain careful records and file on time in subsequent years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will I owe taxes under the Streamlined Filing program?

You may owe some US tax depending on your income and the tax credits available. However, most Americans in Israel owe little or no additional US tax because Israeli tax rates are generally higher than US rates, resulting in sufficient Foreign Tax Credits to offset US liability. You must still pay any balance due when you submit.

Is the Streamlined program still available in 2026?

As of 2026, the IRS Streamlined Filing Compliance Procedures remain available. The program has no announced end date, but the IRS could modify or close it at any time. If you are out of compliance, it is advisable to use the program sooner rather than later to secure the current favorable terms.

Do I need a tax professional for Streamlined Filing?

While not legally required, working with a tax professional experienced in US expat taxation and the streamlined procedures is strongly recommended. The returns must be complete and accurate, the non-willful certification must be carefully worded, and errors could result in the filing being rejected or additional IRS scrutiny.
