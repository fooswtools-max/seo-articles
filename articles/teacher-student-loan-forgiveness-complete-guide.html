Teacher Student Loan Forgiveness Complete Guide

Published: 2026-03-29 | StudLoans | studloans.com

Federal Teacher Loan Forgiveness Program

The Federal Teacher Loan Forgiveness Program offers up to $17,500 in loan forgiveness for qualified teachers who teach for five consecutive years in low-income schools. This program is available for Direct Loans and Federal Family Education Loans (FFEL) originated after October 1, 1998.

Highly qualified teachers in mathematics, science, and special education can receive the maximum $17,500, while teachers in other subjects are eligible for up to $5,000. The school must be listed in the Annual Directory of Designated Low-Income Schools for the Teacher Cancellation Low Income program.

Public Service Loan Forgiveness for Teachers

Teachers working at public schools or qualifying non-profit schools can also pursue Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF), which forgives the remaining balance after 120 qualifying monthly payments while employed full-time in public service.

PSLF has no cap on the forgiveness amount, making it potentially more valuable than the Teacher Loan Forgiveness Program for borrowers with large balances. You must be on an income-driven repayment plan and make all 120 payments while working full-time as a teacher.

Important note: you cannot count the same period of teaching toward both Teacher Loan Forgiveness and PSLF. Strategically, consider which program provides greater benefit based on your loan balance and monthly payment amount.

State-Level Teacher Forgiveness Programs

Many states offer additional loan forgiveness or repayment assistance for teachers. These programs often target critical shortage areas such as STEM education, special education, bilingual education, and rural school districts.

State programs can typically be combined with PSLF, creating a powerful combination that can eliminate debt faster. Research your state's specific offerings through your state education department or higher education authority.

Application Steps and Timeline

For Teacher Loan Forgiveness, submit the Teacher Loan Forgiveness Application after completing five consecutive years of qualifying teaching. Your chief administrative officer must certify your employment for each of the five years.

For PSLF, submit the Employment Certification Form annually and when you change employers. After 120 qualifying payments, submit the PSLF Application for Forgiveness. Track your progress through the PSLF Help Tool on StudentAid.gov.

Keep meticulous records of your employment, payment history, and all correspondence with your loan servicer. Many forgiveness applications have been denied due to documentation issues that could have been prevented with proper record-keeping.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can teachers get both Teacher Loan Forgiveness and PSLF?

Yes, teachers can receive both, but the same teaching period cannot count toward both programs. A common strategy is to use the first five years for Teacher Loan Forgiveness, then continue with PSLF for the remaining balance. This requires careful planning to maximize total forgiveness.

What schools qualify for teacher loan forgiveness?

Qualifying schools must be listed in the Annual Directory of Designated Low-Income Schools. These are typically schools where more than 30% of students qualify for Title I services. Check the Teacher Cancellation Low Income Directory on the Department of Education website for the current list.

How long does it take to get teacher loan forgiveness?

The Teacher Loan Forgiveness Program requires five consecutive complete academic years of qualifying teaching. After completing the service requirement, the application process typically takes 60-120 days. PSLF requires 10 years (120 monthly payments) of qualifying employment and payments.
