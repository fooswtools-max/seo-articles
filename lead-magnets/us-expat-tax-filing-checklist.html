US Expat Tax Filing Checklist

Every form you need: 1040, 2555, 1116, FBAR, 8938, and more

ReturnMyTax — returnmytax.com

US citizens and green card holders must file taxes no matter where they live. Expat tax filing is complex — miss one form and you could face $10,000+ in penalties. Use this checklist to make sure you file everything required.

Core Tax Returns

  • Form 1040 — US Individual Income Tax Return. Required for ALL US citizens/residents regardless of where you live. Report worldwide income including foreign salary, rental income, investments, and self-employment.
  • Form 2555 — Foreign Earned Income Exclusion (FEIE). Exclude up to $126,500 (2026) of foreign earned income from US tax. You must pass either the Physical Presence Test (330 days abroad in 12 months) or the Bona Fide Residence Test.
  • Form 1116 — Foreign Tax Credit. Claim a credit for income taxes paid to a foreign country. Often better than FEIE if your foreign tax rate is higher than the US rate. You can use FEIE + FTC together (for different income).

Foreign Account Reporting

  • FinCEN 114 (FBAR) — Foreign Bank Account Report. Required if your aggregate foreign account balances exceeded $10,000 at ANY point during the year. Filed separately through FinCEN BSA E-Filing. Deadline: April 15 (auto-extension to October 15). Penalty for non-filing: up to $10,000 per account per year (non-willful).
  • Form 8938 — FATCA Statement of Specified Foreign Financial Assets. Required if foreign assets exceed: $200,000 (end of year) or $300,000 (at any point) for expats filing single. Thresholds are higher for married filing jointly. Filed with your 1040.

Additional Forms You May Need

  • Form 8833 — Treaty-Based Return Position. Required if you're claiming benefits under a US tax treaty with your country of residence.
  • Form 3520 — Foreign Trust and Gift Reporting. Required if you received a gift over $100,000 from a foreign person, or have a foreign trust.
  • Form 5471 — Foreign Corporation Ownership. Required if you own 10%+ of a foreign corporation (including your own business abroad).
  • Form 8621 — PFIC (Passive Foreign Investment Company). Required if you hold foreign mutual funds — this is the most punitive form. Consider selling foreign funds and buying US-domiciled equivalents.
  • State tax returns. Some states (California, Virginia, New Mexico, South Carolina) may still require filing even after you've moved abroad. Check your last state of residence.

Penalty Cheat Sheet

FBAR (non-willful): Up to $10,000 per violation per year
FBAR (willful): $100,000 or 50% of account balance, whichever is greater
Form 8938: $10,000 penalty + $10,000/month after 90-day notice
Form 5471: $10,000 per form per year
Form 3520: 5-35% of the amount involved
