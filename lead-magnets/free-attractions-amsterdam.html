Top 10 Free Attractions in Amsterdam

The best free things to do in Amsterdam — no tickets needed

AttractionScout — attractionscout.com

You don't need to spend a fortune to enjoy Amsterdam. Here are 10 incredible attractions and experiences that are completely free (or nearly free). Save your money for the food.

1 Vondelpark

Amsterdam's favorite park — 47 hectares of lakes, lawns, cafes, and open-air theater in summer.

Tip: Rent a bike and cycle through — it's the most Amsterdam thing you can do.

2 Albert Cuyp Market

Amsterdam's largest outdoor market — 260+ stalls with Dutch stroopwafels, cheese, herring, and everything else.

Tip: Try fresh stroopwafel from the stand (warm off the iron) — about 3 euros.

3 Dam Square & Royal Palace (exterior)

The heart of Amsterdam — the Royal Palace, National Monument, and Nieuwe Kerk surround this central square.

Tip: Best as a starting point for exploring the Nine Streets shopping area.

4 Jordaan Neighborhood Walk

Amsterdam's most charming neighborhood — narrow canals, art galleries, brown cafes, and hidden courtyard gardens (hofjes).

Tip: Find the Karthuizerhof — a hidden hofje at Karthuizerstraat 21-39.

5 Canal Ring Walk

UNESCO World Heritage Site — walk along Herengracht, Keizersgracht, and Prinsengracht. Free and beautiful year-round.

Tip: The Golden Bend on Herengracht has the grandest canal houses.

6 Begijnhof

Peaceful hidden courtyard dating to the 14th century with the oldest wooden house in Amsterdam (1528).

Tip: Enter through the gate on Spui square. Be quiet — people live here.

7 NDSM Wharf

Former shipyard turned creative hub — street art, studio spaces, festivals, and a monthly flea market.

Tip: Take the free ferry from behind Central Station. Visit on the first weekend for the IJ-Hallen flea market.

8 Bloemenmarkt (Flower Market)

The world's only floating flower market — tulip bulbs, seeds, and souvenirs on houseboats along Singel canal.

Tip: Buy tulip bulbs only from vendors with an export certificate.

9 Waterlooplein Flea Market

Daily flea market with vintage clothing, vinyl records, antiques, and curiosities since 1886.

Tip: Bargain on prices — especially later in the day when vendors want to pack up.

10 EYE Film Museum (lobby & terrace)

Striking white building across the IJ river. Free ferry, free lobby exhibitions, and the best waterfront terrace in Amsterdam.

Tip: Take the free ferry from behind Centraal Station for great skyline views.

Money-Saving Tips for Amsterdam

Walk everywhere you can — the best discoveries happen on foot.
Eat where locals eat — tourist-area restaurants charge 2-3x more.
Visit paid attractions early — many have free or discounted hours.
Download offline maps — save on data roaming charges.
