Top 10 Free Attractions in Paris

The best free things to do in Paris — no tickets needed

AttractionScout — attractionscout.com

You don't need to spend a fortune to enjoy Paris. Here are 10 incredible attractions and experiences that are completely free (or nearly free). Save your money for the food.

1 Notre-Dame Cathedral (exterior)

Marvel at the Gothic masterpiece from the outside and its ongoing restoration. The plaza and Seine riverbank views are free.

Tip: Best at sunset for golden light on the facade.

2 Sacre-Coeur Basilica

Stunning white basilica at the top of Montmartre with panoramic views of Paris. Entry is free; only the dome climb costs money.

Tip: Arrive early morning to avoid crowds and street vendors.

3 Pere Lachaise Cemetery

The world's most visited cemetery — find Jim Morrison, Oscar Wilde, Edith Piaf, and Chopin. A beautiful, peaceful walk.

Tip: Get a free map at the entrance to find famous graves.

4 Tuileries Garden

Formal French garden between the Louvre and Place de la Concorde. Perfect for a stroll with fountains, statues, and people-watching.

Tip: Grab a crepe from a nearby stand and sit by the octagonal fountain.

5 Le Marais District Walk

One of Paris's oldest neighborhoods with medieval architecture, art galleries, vintage shops, and the beautiful Place des Vosges.

Tip: Visit on Sunday when Rue des Rosiers is bustling with falafel spots.

6 Luxembourg Gardens

Elegant 17th-century garden with the Luxembourg Palace, puppet theater, and model sailboats on the central pond.

Tip: Grab a green chair and relax — it's what Parisians do.

7 Canal Saint-Martin

Charming canal lined with iron footbridges, trendy cafes, and leafy paths. Featured in the film Amelie.

Tip: Walk from Republique to Stalingrad for the full experience.

8 Palais Royal Gardens

Hidden gem right next to the Louvre — serene gardens surrounded by arcaded galleries and Buren's striped columns.

Tip: Combine with a visit to the Louvre courtyard (also free).

9 Free Museum Days (1st Sunday)

The Louvre, Musee d'Orsay, and Centre Pompidou are free on the first Sunday of each month (except summer).

Tip: Arrive before opening — lines are long on free days.

10 Seine River Walk

Walk along the Left Bank from Musee d'Orsay to Notre-Dame. Browse the bouquinistes (booksellers) along the way.

Tip: Best done at twilight when the bridges light up.

Money-Saving Tips for Paris

Walk everywhere you can — the best discoveries happen on foot.
Eat where locals eat — tourist-area restaurants charge 2-3x more.
Visit paid attractions early — many have free or discounted hours.
Download offline maps — save on data roaming charges.
