Sample 10-Day Europe Trip Budgets

Backpacker: $700-1,000 (excluding flights)

Hostels, street food, free walking tours, budget trains, and lots of walking. You'll have an amazing time.

Mid-Range: $1,500-2,500 (excluding flights)

3-star hotels or nice Airbnbs, restaurant meals, paid attractions, city passes, and comfortable trains.

Comfort: $3,000-5,000 (excluding flights)

4-star hotels, fine dining, skip-the-line tours, business class trains, and taxis when needed.

Top 10 Money-Saving Tips

  • Book trains 2-3 months early — London to Paris can be $35 instead of $200.
  • Eat lunch as your big meal — lunch specials are 40-60% cheaper than dinner.
  • Get a city pass ONLY if it saves money — do the math for your specific attractions.
  • Use Revolut or Wise card — no foreign exchange fees, real exchange rate.
  • Walk everywhere possible — European cities are small and beautiful on foot.
  • Cook 2-3 meals at your Airbnb — local markets make this fun, not a chore.
  • Travel on Tuesday-Thursday — flights and trains are cheapest mid-week.
  • Visit Eastern Europe — Prague, Budapest, and Krakow are 50% cheaper than Paris/London.
  • Use free museum days — most major European museums have at least one free day per month.
  • Skip taxis — metro systems in Europe are clean, safe, and much cheaper.
