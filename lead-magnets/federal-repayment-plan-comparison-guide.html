SAVE Plan Deep Dive (Most Generous)

Why SAVE Is Usually the Best IDR Plan

Lowest payments: 5% of discretionary income for undergrad loans (vs 10% for all others)
225% FPL threshold: More income is protected — you pay nothing if earning under ~$33,000
No interest accrual: Unpaid interest doesn't capitalize if you make your payment
Spousal income excluded: If you file taxes separately, spouse's income doesn't count

Decision Flowchart

  • Do you have Parent PLUS loans? Your only IDR option is ICR (must consolidate first).
  • Are you pursuing PSLF? Use SAVE for lowest payments — all IDR plans qualify for PSLF.
  • Is your income low or moderate? SAVE is almost always the best choice.
  • Do you have only undergrad loans? SAVE charges just 5% (half of other plans).
  • Do you expect high salary growth? Consider Standard — pay more now, save on interest.
  • Want the lowest total cost? Standard 10-year plan minimizes total interest paid.
