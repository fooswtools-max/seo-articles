The 4% Rule Quick Reference

How Much Do You Need to Retire?

Multiply your desired annual retirement income by 25.

Want $50,000/year? You need $1,250,000 saved.
Want $80,000/year? You need $2,000,000 saved.
Want $100,000/year? You need $2,500,000 saved.

This assumes 4% annual withdrawals, historically sustainable for 30+ years.

Key Ages to Remember

  • Age 50: Catch-up contributions begin ($7,500 extra for 401(k), $1,000 for IRA)
  • Age 55: Can withdraw from current employer 401(k) penalty-free if separated from service
  • Age 59.5: Penalty-free withdrawals from IRAs and 401(k)s begin
  • Age 60-63: Super catch-up contributions ($11,250 extra for 401(k))
  • Age 62: Earliest Social Security eligibility (reduced benefit)
  • Age 65: Medicare eligibility begins
  • Age 67: Full Social Security retirement age (for those born after 1960)
  • Age 70: Maximum Social Security benefit (delayed credits stop)
  • Age 73: Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) begin for traditional accounts
