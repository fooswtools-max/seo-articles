Top 10 Free Attractions in Bali

The best free things to do in Bali — no tickets needed

AttractionScout — attractionscout.com

You don't need to spend a fortune to enjoy Bali. Here are 10 incredible attractions and experiences that are completely free (or nearly free). Save your money for the food.

1 Tanah Lot Temple (exterior)

Iconic sea temple perched on a rock offshore. The surrounding cliffs and sunset views are free; temple entry is small.

Tip: Arrive 1 hour before sunset for golden light. The temple is accessible at low tide.

2 Tegallalang Rice Terraces (viewpoint)

Stunning tiered rice paddies — the main viewpoints from the road are free. Walking the terraces may cost a small donation.

Tip: Visit early morning (7-8am) for the best light and zero crowds.

3 Ubud Monkey Forest

Sacred forest with 700+ monkeys, ancient temples, and giant banyan trees. Entry is about $5 (barely a cost).

Tip: Don't bring food, sunglasses, or dangling accessories — monkeys will grab them.

4 Seminyak Beach Sunset

Bali's best sunset beach — golden sand, beach bars, and surfers silhouetted against fiery skies. Free.

Tip: Grab a beanbag at a beach bar — you only need to buy a drink to use it.

5 Ubud Art Market

Traditional market with handmade crafts, paintings, and textiles. Free to browse. Bargaining is expected.

Tip: Start at 50% of the asking price and negotiate from there. Morning prices are better.

6 Campuhan Ridge Walk

Scenic 2 km ridge walk through grass-covered hills between two river valleys. Free and stunning.

Tip: Go at sunrise (6am) for cool temperatures and magical golden light.

7 Pura Tirta Empul (spring temple area)

Sacred water temple where Balinese do purification rituals. The outer areas and gardens are beautiful.

Tip: You can participate in the purification ritual — wear a sarong (available to rent).

8 Echo Beach (Canggu)

Surfer beach with dramatic waves, black sand, and clifftop temples. Free to enjoy, cheap coconuts from vendors.

Tip: Watch the surf competitions and sunset with a 20k IDR ($1.25) coconut.

9 Goa Gajah (Elephant Cave) area

Ancient cave with carved entrance near Ubud. The surrounding gardens, bathing pools, and rice paddies are scenic.

Tip: Combine with nearby Yeh Pulu rock carvings — a hidden gem with almost no tourists.

10 Uluwatu Temple area

Clifftop temple with ocean views and the famous Kecak fire dance at sunset (dance has a small fee).

Tip: Watch for monkeys — they steal sunglasses and phones. The cliff views alone are worth the visit.

Money-Saving Tips for Bali

Walk everywhere you can — the best discoveries happen on foot.
Eat where locals eat — tourist-area restaurants charge 2-3x more.
Visit paid attractions early — many have free or discounted hours.
Download offline maps — save on data roaming charges.
