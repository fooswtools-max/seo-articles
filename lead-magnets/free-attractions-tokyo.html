Top 10 Free Attractions in Tokyo

The best free things to do in Tokyo — no tickets needed

AttractionScout — attractionscout.com

You don't need to spend a fortune to enjoy Tokyo. Here are 10 incredible attractions and experiences that are completely free (or nearly free). Save your money for the food.

1 Senso-ji Temple (Asakusa)

Tokyo's oldest temple (628 AD) with the iconic Kaminarimon gate and Nakamise shopping street. Free entry.

Tip: Visit before 7am or after 8pm when the temple is illuminated and empty.

2 Meiji Shrine

Serene Shinto shrine in a 170-acre forest in the middle of Harajuku. Free entry — experience a traditional prayer.

Tip: Walk the forested path from Harajuku station. Watch for traditional wedding processions on weekends.

3 Tsukiji Outer Market

The famous inner market moved to Toyosu, but the free outer market remains — 400+ shops with street food.

Tip: Try tamagoyaki (Japanese omelette on a stick) and fresh sashimi for breakfast.

4 Harajuku & Takeshita Street

Tokyo's youth culture epicenter — wild fashion, crepe shops, and people-watching. Free and always entertaining.

Tip: Visit on Sunday for the best people-watching and cosplay.

5 Shinjuku Gyoen National Garden

Stunning garden with Japanese, English, and French sections. Entry is just 500 yen — basically free.

Tip: Visit during cherry blossom season (late March-early April) for unforgettable views.

6 Shibuya Crossing

The world's busiest pedestrian crossing — 3,000 people cross at once. Free to walk and photograph.

Tip: Watch from the Starbucks on the 2nd floor of Tsutaya building for the best view.

7 Akihabara Electric Town

Anime, manga, electronics, and gaming paradise. Free to browse the multi-story arcades and shops.

Tip: Visit Don Quijote for crazy souvenirs and the top-floor arcade.

8 Tokyo Metropolitan Government Building

Free observation deck on the 45th floor with views of Mount Fuji on clear days. Much better than paid Tokyo Tower.

Tip: Go on a clear winter day for the best Mt. Fuji views. North tower is usually less crowded.

9 Ueno Park

Large public park with shrines, museums (some free), Shinobazu Pond, and Tokyo's best cherry blossom viewing.

Tip: The park connects to Ameyoko market — a bustling discount shopping street.

10 Yanaka Old Town

Tokyo's best-preserved old neighborhood — traditional houses, cat-themed shops, historic cemetery, and the famous Yanaka Ginza shopping street.

Tip: Start at Nippori Station and walk downhill through the cemetery to Yanaka Ginza.

Money-Saving Tips for Tokyo

Walk everywhere you can — the best discoveries happen on foot.
Eat where locals eat — tourist-area restaurants charge 2-3x more.
Visit paid attractions early — many have free or discounted hours.
Download offline maps — save on data roaming charges.
