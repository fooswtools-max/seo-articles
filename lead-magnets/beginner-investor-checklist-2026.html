2026 Beginner Investor Checklist

10 essential steps to complete before your first investment

AI How To Invest — aihowtoinvest.com

Starting to invest can feel overwhelming. This checklist breaks down the 10 essential steps every beginner should complete before putting money into the market. Check each one off as you go.

Before You Invest

  • Step 1: Build a 3-6 month emergency fund. Keep this in a high-yield savings account (not invested). You need a safety net before risking any money in the market. Aim for 3 months if you have stable income, 6 if variable.
  • Step 2: Pay off high-interest debt first. Any debt above 7% interest (credit cards, personal loans) should be eliminated before investing. The stock market averages ~10% annually — paying off a 22% APR card is a guaranteed better return.
  • Step 3: Define your investment goal and timeline. Retirement in 30 years? House down payment in 5 years? Your timeline determines your risk tolerance and which accounts to use.

Setting Up Your Accounts

  • Step 4: Open a tax-advantaged account first. Max out your 401(k) match (free money), then open a Roth IRA ($7,000 limit in 2026). Only after these are funded should you use a taxable brokerage account.
  • Step 5: Choose a low-cost brokerage. Look for: $0 commissions, no account minimums, fractional shares, and a good mobile app. Fidelity, Schwab, and Vanguard are top choices.
  • Step 6: Start with broad index funds. A total stock market index fund (like VTI or FXAIX) gives you instant diversification across 3,000+ companies for under 0.04% in fees. This is the foundation.

Making Your First Investment

  • Step 7: Set up automatic contributions. Automate a fixed amount on each payday. This removes emotion from investing and uses dollar-cost averaging to smooth out market volatility.
  • Step 8: Understand your asset allocation. A simple rule: subtract your age from 110 for your stock percentage. Age 30 = 80% stocks, 20% bonds. Use a target-date fund if you want it fully automated.
  • Step 9: Don't check your portfolio daily. Markets go up and down. Checking daily leads to panic selling. Set a quarterly review schedule and rebalance once a year.
  • Step 10: Keep learning — but keep it simple. Avoid stock tips, crypto hype, and "get rich quick" schemes. The boring strategy (index funds, consistent contributions, long time horizon) outperforms 90% of professional fund managers.

Key Numbers to Remember

$7,000 — 2026 Roth IRA contribution limit ($8,000 if 50+)
$23,500 — 2026 401(k) contribution limit ($31,000 if 50+)
0.03-0.10% — Target expense ratio for index funds
10-15% — Recommended percentage of income to invest
