PSLF Eligibility Checklist

Step-by-step guide to qualifying for Public Service Loan Forgiveness

StudLoans — studloans.com

Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF) can erase your entire remaining student loan balance after 120 qualifying payments. But the details matter — one wrong step and your payments may not count. Use this checklist to verify you qualify.

Employer Eligibility

  • You work for a qualifying employer. Government (federal, state, local, tribal), 501(c)(3) nonprofits, AmeriCorps, Peace Corps. For-profit companies NEVER qualify, even if doing "public service" work.
  • You work full-time (30+ hours/week). Part-time at two qualifying employers can count if combined hours are 30+.
  • Submit the PSLF Employment Certification Form annually. Don't wait until 120 payments — submit every year or when you change employers. This catches problems early.

Loan Eligibility

  • You have Direct federal loans ONLY. FFEL and Perkins loans do NOT qualify unless you consolidate them into a Direct Consolidation Loan. Private loans never qualify.
  • Check your loan types on studentaid.gov. Log in and look for "Direct" in the loan name. If you see "FFEL" — consolidate first.
  • If you consolidated, your payment count resets to zero. Plan accordingly — consolidation is required for FFEL loans but restarts your clock.

Payment Requirements

  • You are on an income-driven repayment (IDR) plan. SAVE, PAYE, IBR, or ICR. The Standard 10-year plan technically qualifies but you'd pay off the loan before reaching 120 payments.
  • You've made 120 qualifying monthly payments. Payments must be made after October 1, 2007. They don't need to be consecutive.
  • Each payment was made on time (within 15 days of due date). Late payments don't count toward your 120.
  • Each payment was for the full amount due. Partial payments don't count. $0 payments on IDR plans DO count if that's your calculated amount.

After 120 Payments

  • Submit the PSLF application (same form as employment certification). MOHELA is the PSLF servicer — your loans should already be transferred to them.
  • Continue making payments while your application is processed. Processing takes 3-6 months. Overpayments will be refunded after approval.
  • The forgiven amount is tax-free. Unlike IDR forgiveness, PSLF forgiveness is not treated as taxable income. This is a huge advantage — $200,000 forgiven = $200,000 saved (no tax bill).

PSLF by the Numbers

120 — qualifying payments required (10 years)
30+ — hours/week for full-time status
$0 — tax on forgiven balance
10 years — minimum timeline (fastest path to forgiveness)
946,000+ — borrowers approved for PSLF as of 2026
