Eiffel Tower vs Arc de Triomphe: Which Paris Attraction Is Better?

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Trying to decide between Eiffel Tower and Arc de Triomphe in Paris? Both are top attractions, but they offer very different experiences. This comparison breaks down cost, time needed, best for whom, and our verdict to help you choose (or visit both).

Eiffel Tower: What to Expect

Iconic iron tower with panoramic city views from three observation levels. Eiffel Tower is one of the most popular attractions in Paris and attracts millions of visitors each year. Tickets cost approximately $29 per adult.

Visitors typically spend 1.5 to 3 hours here. We recommend arriving early in the morning or late in the afternoon to avoid the largest crowds. Book skip-the-line tickets online in advance to save time.

Arc de Triomphe: What to Expect

Monumental arch with rooftop terrace overlooking the Champs-Elysees. Arc de Triomphe offers a different but equally rewarding experience in Paris. Tickets cost approximately $16 per adult.

Plan for 1.5 to 3 hours to fully appreciate what Arc de Triomphe has to offer. Like most popular Paris attractions, booking in advance is strongly recommended.

Price Comparison

Arc de Triomphe is the more affordable option, saving you approximately $13 per person.

Both attractions offer excellent value for the experience. If budget is a concern, consider purchasing a city pass or combo ticket that includes both at a discount.

Our Verdict: Which Should You Choose?

If you only have time for one, choose Eiffel Tower if you prefer landmark experiences, or Arc de Triomphe if you lean toward landmark attractions. But honestly? If your schedule allows, visit both — they complement each other perfectly and together give you a well-rounded Paris experience.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the must-see attractions in Paris?

The top attractions include the Eiffel Tower, Louvre Museum, Notre-Dame Cathedral, Sacre-Coeur, Musee d'Orsay, and the Palace of Versailles as a day trip. Paris also offers incredible food tours, Seine river cruises, and neighborhood walks.

How many days do you need in Paris?

3-5 days is ideal for first-time visitors. This allows time for major landmarks, a museum day, a food tour, and a day trip to Versailles or Giverny.

Is Paris expensive for tourists?

Paris can be moderate to expensive. Many top museums offer free entry for under-26 EU residents. Free attractions include the Sacre-Coeur, Notre-Dame exterior, parks, and walking neighborhoods like Le Marais and Montmartre.
