The Month Most People Visit Amsterdam Is Actually the Worst

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Planning a trip to Amsterdam, Netherlands? The best time to visit Amsterdam is April-May and September-October when you get pleasant weather, manageable crowds, and the best combination of activities and prices.

Month-by-Month Guide to Amsterdam

Peak Season (Summer)

Summer brings the longest days and warmest weather, but also the biggest crowds and highest prices. If visiting in peak season, book accommodations 2-3 months in advance and buy attraction tickets online to skip long lines.

Shoulder Season (Spring & Fall)

This is our recommended time to visit. You get comfortable temperatures, fewer tourists, lower hotel prices, and full access to all attractions. April-May and September-October offers the ideal balance of weather and value.

Off-Season (Winter)

Winter means the fewest tourists and lowest prices, but shorter days and potentially cold or rainy weather. Some attractions may have reduced hours. However, holiday markets and festive atmosphere can make winter visits magical.

Top Attractions in Amsterdam

  • Anne Frank House — The secret annex where Anne Frank hid during WWII and wrote her diary
  • Rijksmuseum — Dutch Golden Age masterpieces including Rembrandt's Night Watch
  • Van Gogh Museum — The world's largest collection of Van Gogh paintings and letters
  • Canal Cruise — Scenic boat tour through Amsterdam's UNESCO-listed canal ring
  • Heineken Experience — Interactive brewery tour with beer tasting in the original Heineken facility

Budget Tip

The average daily budget for Amsterdam is $100-200 per person, including accommodation, food, transport, and attractions. Visit during shoulder season to save 20-30% on hotels.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the must-see attractions in Amsterdam?

Top attractions include the Anne Frank House, Rijksmuseum, Van Gogh Museum, a canal cruise, Vondelpark, and exploring the Jordaan neighborhood. The Heineken Experience and Albert Cuyp Market are also popular.

How do I get tickets to the Anne Frank House?

Tickets are released online about 6 weeks in advance and sell out quickly. Check the official website on the release date. A small number of tickets are released each day for same-day visits.

Is Amsterdam walkable?

Amsterdam is very walkable and even more bicycle-friendly. Most major attractions are within 30 minutes walk of each other. Renting a bike is the most authentic way to explore the city.
