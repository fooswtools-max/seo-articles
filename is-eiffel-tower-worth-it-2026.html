Is Eiffel Tower Worth It? (2026 Honest Review)

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Is the Eiffel Tower Worth Visiting in 2026?

Verdict: Absolutely yes. Despite being the most visited paid monument in the world, the Eiffel Tower delivers an experience that justifies the cost and wait time for most travelers. The panoramic views of Paris from the top are genuinely breathtaking and unlike anything else available in the city.

What You Actually Get

  • 1st Floor: Glass floor panels, restaurant, exhibits on the tower's history
  • 2nd Floor: The best photo spot — high enough for panoramic views, close enough to see details
  • Summit (276m): Gustave Eiffel's restored office, champagne bar, 360-degree views up to 65km

How to Skip the Lines

The biggest complaint about the Eiffel Tower is the wait time. Here's how to minimize it:

  1. Book tickets online 60 days ahead — timed-entry slots sell out fast
  2. Take the stairs to the 2nd floor — the stair line is usually 70% shorter
  3. Visit at 9 AM opening — crowds build from 11 AM onwards
  4. Consider a sunset visit (7-8 PM in summer) — magical lighting but book ahead
Pro tip: If you cannot get summit tickets online, go to the 2nd floor first and buy summit tickets there. The line is shorter and availability is often better.

Pros and Cons

Drawbacks

  • Long wait times without advance booking

Alternatives If You Skip It

If you decide the Eiffel Tower is not for you, consider these viewpoints:

  • Montparnasse Tower (17 EUR) — actually includes the Eiffel Tower in the view
  • Sacre-Coeur Basilica (free) — stunning hilltop panorama of Paris
  • Arc de Triomphe rooftop (13 EUR) — Champs-Elysees views included

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Eiffel Tower worth the price?

Yes, for most visitors the 17-27 EUR ticket provides exceptional value. The panoramic views, historical significance, and unique experience make it one of the better-priced major attractions in Europe compared to places like the London Eye (37 GBP) or Empire State Building ($44 USD).

How long should I spend at the Eiffel Tower?

Plan 1.5-2 hours including security and elevator wait times. If you take the stairs to the 2nd floor, add 30 minutes for the climb (674 steps). Evening visits may take longer due to crowds.

Is the summit worth the extra cost?

If it is a clear day, absolutely. The summit adds about 10 EUR over the 2nd floor price and offers views up to 65km. On cloudy days, the 2nd floor provides a better experience since you are close enough to identify landmarks.

Can I visit the Eiffel Tower for free?

You can visit the ground level and Champ de Mars park for free, which offers great photo opportunities. The esplanade beneath the tower is free to access. Only the upper levels require tickets.
