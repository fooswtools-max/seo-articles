Best Budget Routes

Route 1: Eastern Europe Circuit (14 days, ~$700-900 total)

  1. Prague (3 nights) — free walking tours, cheap beer, stunning architecture
  2. Vienna (2 nights) — train from Prague (4h, $15-25 with RegioJet)
  3. Budapest (3 nights) — thermal baths, ruin bars, incredible food value
  4. Krakow (2 nights) — bus from Budapest (6h, $15-20)
  5. Warsaw (2 nights) — train from Krakow (2.5h, $10-15)

Route 2: Mediterranean (14 days, ~$900-1200 total)

  1. Barcelona (3 nights) — beaches, free Gaudi views, La Boqueria Market
  2. Nice/Monaco (2 nights) — budget bus from Barcelona (8h, $20-30)
  3. Rome (3 nights) — fly from Nice ($30-50 budget airlines)
  4. Naples/Amalfi (2 nights) — train from Rome (1h, $10-15)
  5. Athens (3 nights) — fly from Naples ($40-60)

Money-Saving Strategies

  • Accommodation: Mix hostels ($15-35/night), Couchsurfing (free), and house-sitting (free + access to full kitchen)
  • Food: Cook 1 meal/day in hostel kitchens, eat lunch specials (many restaurants offer 3-course lunch for $8-12), shop at local markets
  • Transport: Book trains/buses 4-6 weeks ahead via Omio or Trainline. Budget airlines (Ryanair, EasyJet) beat trains on longer routes
  • Activities: Free walking tours (tip-based), free museum days, parks and viewpoints, and church visits are always free
  • Money: Use Wise or Revolut for zero-fee currency conversion. Avoid airport exchange kiosks. Always pay in local currency.

Packing for Budget Travel

  • One carry-on bag (avoid checked bag fees: $30-60 per flight)
  • Quick-dry clothing that can be hand-washed
  • Hostel essentials: padlock, eye mask, ear plugs, microfiber towel
  • Reusable water bottle (tap water is safe in most of Western Europe)
  • EU power adapter and portable charger
  • Photocopy of passport and travel insurance on phone
