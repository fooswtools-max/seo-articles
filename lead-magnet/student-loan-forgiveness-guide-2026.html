Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF)

Eligibility Requirements

  • Work full-time (30+ hours/week) for a qualifying employer (government, 501(c)(3) nonprofit)
  • Have Direct Loans (consolidate if you have FFEL or Perkins)
  • Be on an Income-Driven Repayment plan
  • Make 120 qualifying monthly payments (do not need to be consecutive)

Step-by-Step Application

  1. Verify your employer at studentaid.gov/pslf
  2. Submit Employment Certification Form (ECF) annually
  3. Switch to an IDR plan if not already on one
  4. Consolidate non-Direct loans into a Direct Consolidation Loan
  5. Track qualifying payments on studentaid.gov
  6. Apply for forgiveness after 120 qualifying payments
Critical change in 2024-2026: The SAVE plan counts as qualifying for PSLF and may offer the lowest payments. If you are pursuing PSLF, switching to SAVE could maximize your forgiveness amount.

SAVE Plan (Saving on a Valuable Education)

  • Payment cap: 5% of discretionary income for undergrad loans (down from 10%)
  • Interest subsidy: Government covers any unpaid interest — your balance never grows
  • Forgiveness: After 20 years (undergrad) or 25 years (grad). Borrowers with original balances of $12,000 or less get forgiveness after just 10 years.
  • Example: $40k salary, $35k undergrad loans = ~$53/month payment instead of $394 on standard plan

Teacher Loan Forgiveness

  • Teach 5 consecutive years in a Title I school
  • Up to $17,500 forgiven for math, science, and special education teachers
  • Up to $5,000 forgiven for other subjects
  • Can be combined with PSLF (use Teacher Forgiveness first, then PSLF for remaining balance)

Forgiveness Checklist

  • Identify which forgiveness programs you qualify for
  • Consolidate non-Direct loans if needed
  • Switch to the optimal IDR plan (usually SAVE)
  • Submit Employment Certification Form annually
  • Track qualifying payment count on studentaid.gov
  • Set calendar reminder to reapply for IDR every 12 months
  • Keep all employment records and payment confirmations
  • Apply for forgiveness when eligible — it is not automatic
