Step 2: Open the Right Accounts (Priority Order)

  1. 401(k) up to employer match — free money, always max this first
  2. HSA (if eligible) — triple tax advantage: deductible, grows tax-free, tax-free withdrawals for medical
  3. Roth IRA ($7,000/year limit in 2026) — tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement
  4. Max out 401(k) ($23,500 limit in 2026) — reduces taxable income now
  5. Taxable brokerage — no contribution limits, flexible access

Step 3: Choose Your Portfolio

The Simple Portfolio: A target-date fund matching your retirement year (e.g., Vanguard Target Retirement 2055) handles everything automatically. One fund, zero maintenance.

Step 4: Automate Everything

  • Set up automatic payroll deductions to 401(k)
  • Schedule automatic monthly transfers to IRA
  • Enable automatic dividend reinvestment (DRIP)
  • Set annual reminder to increase contribution by 1%
  • Rebalance portfolio once per year (or use target-date fund)

Step 5: The Withdrawal Strategy

The 4% Rule: In your first year of retirement, withdraw 4% of your portfolio. Adjust for inflation each year after. This strategy has a 95% success rate over 30-year periods historically.

Key Milestones to Track

  • Age 50: Catch-up contributions become available ($7,500 extra to 401k)
  • Age 55: Access 401(k) penalty-free if you leave your employer
  • Age 59.5: Penalty-free withdrawals from retirement accounts
  • Age 62: Earliest Social Security eligibility (reduced benefits)
  • Age 65: Medicare eligibility
  • Age 67: Full Social Security retirement age
  • Age 70: Maximum Social Security benefit (delayed credits)
  • Age 73: Required Minimum Distributions begin
