Complete Paris Travel Checklist

Everything you need to plan the perfect Paris trip in 2026

3 Months Before: Planning

  • Set your budget ($150-300/day for mid-range including accommodation)
  • Book flights (cheapest: Jan-Mar, Sep-Nov; most expensive: Jun-Aug, Dec)
  • Apply for or renew passport (6+ months validity required)
  • Research arrondissements to choose where to stay
  • Book accommodation (Le Marais, Saint-Germain, and Montmartre are top picks)

1 Month Before: Booking

  • Book Eiffel Tower tickets (timed entry, sells out fast)
  • Reserve Musee d'Orsay and Louvre skip-the-line tickets
  • Book Versailles tickets if doing a day trip
  • Purchase Paris Museum Pass (2/4/6 day) if visiting 3+ museums
  • Download Citymapper app for Metro navigation
  • Buy Navigo Easy card online for public transport
  • Book 1-2 special dinners (Le Bouillon Chartier for budget, Le Jules Verne for splurge)

1 Week Before: Preparation

  • Notify bank of travel dates to prevent card blocks
  • Check weather forecast and adjust packing
  • Download offline Google Maps for Paris
  • Print hotel confirmation and emergency contacts
  • Learn 10 basic French phrases (bonjour, merci, l'addition s'il vous plait)
  • Pack a portable charger and EU adapter (Type C/E plug)

Neighborhood Quick Guide

  • 1st-4th arr. (Le Marais / Islands): Central, walkable to everything, historic — best for first-timers
  • 5th-6th arr. (Latin Quarter / Saint-Germain): Intellectual Paris, bookshops, cafes — best for couples
  • 7th arr. (Eiffel Tower): Quiet, residential, great views — best for families
  • 9th-10th arr. (Opera / Canal Saint-Martin): Trendy, local feel, good food — best for repeat visitors
  • 18th arr. (Montmartre): Bohemian, artistic, hilltop views — best for atmosphere
