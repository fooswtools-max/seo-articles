Rome on a Budget: Free & Cheap Things to Do in 2026

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Think Rome is too expensive? Think again. With the right planning, you can experience the best of Rome, Italy without breaking the bank. This guide covers free attractions, cheap eats, money-saving transport tips, and budget accommodation.

Free Things to Do in Rome

  • St. Peter's Basilica (Free) — World's largest church with Michelangelo's dome (dome climb: 10 euros)
  • Trevi Fountain (Free) — Baroque masterpiece where tradition says tossing a coin ensures your return

Many of Rome's best experiences cost nothing — walking through historic neighborhoods, enjoying parks, people-watching at famous squares, and exploring street markets.

Cheap Attractions Under $15

  • Pantheon ($5) — Perfectly preserved 2,000-year-old Roman temple with iconic oculus
  • Borghese Gallery ($15) — Intimate museum with Bernini sculptures and Caravaggio paintings

Budget Food Tips

  • Street food and markets: The cheapest and often most authentic meals
  • Lunch specials: Many restaurants offer set lunch menus at 30-50% less than dinner
  • Supermarkets: Pick up breakfast supplies and snacks at local grocery stores
  • Water bottle: Carry a reusable bottle to avoid buying drinks at tourist prices
  • Eat where locals eat: Venture two blocks from any major attraction for better prices

Money-Saving Transport Tips

  • Day passes: Buy a public transport day pass instead of individual tickets
  • Walk: Most city centers are walkable — you will see more and save money
  • Avoid taxis from the airport: Use public transport or pre-booked shuttles
  • Bike rental: Many cities have affordable bike-sharing programs

Budget Accommodation Tips

  • Stay slightly outside the center: Hotels 2-3 metro stops from the center are 30-50% cheaper
  • Book 4-6 weeks ahead: Best balance of availability and price
  • Consider hostels with private rooms: Often $30-50/night with hotel-like privacy
  • Apartments with kitchens: Save on meals by cooking breakfast at home

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the top things to do in Rome?

Must-sees include the Colosseum, Vatican Museums and Sistine Chapel, St. Peter's Basilica, Trevi Fountain, Pantheon, and the Roman Forum. Food tours in Trastevere and day trips to Pompeii are also popular.

How do I avoid crowds at the Colosseum?

Book timed-entry tickets online in advance. Early morning (8:30 AM opening) or late afternoon slots have fewer crowds. The Roma Pass includes skip-the-line access. Consider a guided underground tour for a unique experience.

How many days do you need in Rome?

3-4 days covers the major attractions comfortably. Add a 5th day for a day trip to Pompeii, Tivoli, or the Amalfi Coast.
