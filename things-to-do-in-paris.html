Best Things to Do in Paris - 2026 Guide

Updated April 2026 · AttractionScout

Paris is the romantic capital of France, known for art, cuisine, and iconic landmarks. Whether you are planning your first visit or returning for more, this guide covers the 8 best things to do in Paris, France including prices, tips, and what to expect at each attraction.

Top 8 Things to Do in Paris

1. Eiffel Tower (~$29)

Iconic iron tower with panoramic city views from three observation levels. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

2. Louvre Museum (~$22)

World's largest art museum, home to the Mona Lisa and 35,000+ works. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

3. Musee d'Orsay (~$16)

Impressionist masterpieces in a stunning converted railway station. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

4. Palace of Versailles (~$21)

Opulent royal palace with Hall of Mirrors and formal gardens. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

5. Sacre-Coeur (Free)

White-domed basilica atop Montmartre with sweeping Paris views. Free to visit — just show up and enjoy.

6. Arc de Triomphe (~$16)

Monumental arch with rooftop terrace overlooking the Champs-Elysees. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

7. Seine River Cruise (~$16)

Scenic boat ride past Notre-Dame, Eiffel Tower, and historic bridges. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

8. Catacombs of Paris (~$29)

Underground ossuary containing the remains of six million people. Book tickets online in advance to skip the line.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Paris is April-June and September-October when weather is pleasant and crowds are manageable.

Money-Saving Tips

  • Look for a Paris city pass that bundles popular attractions at a discount
  • Book skip-the-line tickets online to save both time and money
  • Visit free attractions and parks to balance your budget
  • Eat at local spots away from the main tourist streets

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the must-see attractions in Paris?

The top attractions include the Eiffel Tower, Louvre Museum, Notre-Dame Cathedral, Sacre-Coeur, Musee d'Orsay, and the Palace of Versailles as a day trip. Paris also offers incredible food tours, Seine river cruises, and neighborhood walks.

How many days do you need in Paris?

3-5 days is ideal for first-time visitors. This allows time for major landmarks, a museum day, a food tour, and a day trip to Versailles or Giverny.

Is Paris expensive for tourists?

Paris can be moderate to expensive. Many top museums offer free entry for under-26 EU residents. Free attractions include the Sacre-Coeur, Notre-Dame exterior, parks, and walking neighborhoods like Le Marais and Montmartre.
